Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unearth the Classics and Discover Where to Buy Them

Hello there, fellow retro game enthusiasts! Are you ready to take a walk down memory lane with me? Let's go on a journey to where the magic of handheld gaming started and find out where you can buy these gems. (Psst, no need to thank me, I'm just doing my duty as your friendly neighborhood blogger!)

1. Game Boy

Does anyone else remember waking up early on Saturday mornings, not to watch cartoons, but to play games on your Game Boy under the blanket? Ah, those were simpler times. Nintendo's Game Boy was the first handheld gaming console many of us ever owned. With its iconic green screen and its legendary game library (Pokemon, anyone?), the Game Boy is a must-have for any retro gaming collection.

2. Game Boy Advance

Nintendo continued their handheld legacy with the Game Boy Advance. I mean, who among us didn't spend countless hours on Super Mario Advance? And don't get me started on the epicness of The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap. It's like a ticket back to the early 2000s!

3. Sega Game Gear

Oh, the Sega Game Gear, the Game Boy's arch-nemesis. This was Sega's answer to Nintendo's dominance in the handheld market. Color screen? Check. Backlit screen? Check. Eating batteries for breakfast? Double check! But we loved it anyway, didn't we?

4. Neo Geo Pocket

SNK's Neo Geo Pocket was like the cool kid on the block that nobody really knew about. Despite its short lifespan, this handheld was home to some great titles, including a portable version of King of Fighters. Talk about a hidden gem!

5. Atari Lynx

Remember the Atari Lynx? Yeah, neither does anyone else. But that doesn't make it any less of a retro classic. As the first handheld with a color screen, the Lynx was ahead of its time. If only it had better games, oh well.

6. Playstation Portable (PSP)

The PSP was Sony's first foray into the handheld gaming world, and boy, did they make a splash! Who can forget playing God of War whilst on a long car journey? Or having a quick race on Ridge Racer during school breaks? Ah, to be young again.

7. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was the Game Boy's cooler, younger cousin with its dual screens and touchscreen functionality. From Brain Age to Animal Crossing, the DS had something for everyone. And who could forget PictoChat? Now, that was social networking before social networking was cool.

8. Game and Watch

Before there was the Game Boy, there was the Game and Watch. These handhelds were simple, yet addictive. They weren't just games, they were also... watches! Who wouldn't want to tell the time and play Donkey Kong at the same time?

9. Wonderswan

The Wonderswan was Bandai's contribution to the handheld market. While it never made it big outside of Japan, it managed to hold its own against the Game Boy thanks to its exclusive Final Fantasy titles. So, if you're a Final Fantasy fanatic, you might want to hunt one of these down.

10. TurboExpress

Last but not least, we have the TurboExpress, the handheld version of the TurboGrafx-16. This thing was a beast, not only because it could play the same games as its home console counterpart, but also because it cost a small fortune. But hey, who said being a gamer was cheap?

There you have it, folks. Ten of the best retro gaming handhelds that will make you wish you could travel back in time. Sure, finding these classics may take some digging, but trust me, the thrill of the hunt is all part of the fun. So, are you ready to start your retro gaming collection? Let the games (hunting) begin!

And remember, no matter how advanced gaming technology gets, there's always a special place in our hearts (and our dusty old game drawers) for these retro gaming handhelds. Because, as we all know, old is gold.
