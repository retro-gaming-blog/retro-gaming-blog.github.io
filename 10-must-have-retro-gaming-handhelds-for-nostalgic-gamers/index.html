10 Must-Have Retro Gaming Handhelds for Nostalgic Gamers

Are you a nostalgic gamer who longs for the good old days of pixelated graphics and simple gameplay? Do you miss blowing into cartridges and smacking your console when it wouldn't cooperate? Well, fear not, my fellow retro gaming enthusiasts! In this blog post, I have compiled a list of 10 must-have retro gaming handhelds that will transport you back to the golden era of gaming. So, grab your favorite bag of chips and get ready for a trip down memory lane!

1. Game Boy:

Ah, the classic Game Boy! Who could forget this iconic handheld device? With its monochrome display and chunky design, it was the epitome of cool back in the day. Whether you were battling Pokémon, saving the princess in Super Mario Land, or trying to catch that elusive Tetris high score, the Game Boy was always there for you. It was the perfect companion for long car rides or when you wanted to hide from your annoying siblings in the bathroom.

2. Sega Game Gear:

If you were more of a Sega fan, then the Game Gear was your go-to handheld. With its full-color backlit screen, it was like having a tiny arcade machine in your hands. Sure, it ate through batteries faster than a hungry Pikachu, but the hours of entertainment it provided were worth every penny. Plus, it had some killer exclusives like Sonic the Hedgehog and Shinobi. Who needs sleep when you can stay up all night with your trusty Game Gear?

3. Atari Lynx:

Okay, let's be honest here. The Atari Lynx was a bit of a weirdo. It was big, it was clunky, and it probably wouldn't fit in your pocket unless you were wearing MC Hammer pants. But hey, it had color and backlighting before anyone else did! And let's not forget the sheer joy of playing classics like California Games and Chip's Challenge on the go. The Lynx may not have been the most popular handheld, but it definitely had its charm.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color:

Ah, the Neo Geo Pocket Color. If you were a hardcore gamer back in the day, chances are you lusted after this bad boy. With its crisp color display and impressive library of games, it was the Rolls-Royce of handhelds. Sure, it may have been a bit on the expensive side, but can you really put a price on the satisfaction of obliterating your friends in a heated game of Metal Slug? Didn't think so.

5. Nintendo DS:

Now, I know what you're thinking. The Nintendo DS isn't technically retro. But hear me out! The DS was released over 15 years ago, which means it's officially old enough to be considered retro. Plus, it had some amazing games that are now considered classics, like New Super Mario Bros. and The Legend of Zelda: Phantom Hourglass. So, let's just pretend it's retro, okay?

6. Sony PSP:

Ah, the Sony PSP. It was like having a mini PlayStation in your hands. With its sleek design and impressive graphics, it was the envy of all your friends. And let's not forget the joy of playing games like Grand Theft Auto: Liberty City Stories and Metal Gear Solid: Peace Walker on the go. Who needs a social life when you have a PSP?

7. Game Boy Advance:

If you were a true Game Boy fan, then the Game Boy Advance was a must-have. With its improved graphics and backward compatibility with Game Boy and Game Boy Color games, it was a dream come true. Who could resist the allure of playing classics like Pokémon FireRed, The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap, and Advance Wars? Not me, that's for sure!

8. Sega Nomad:

Now, here's a handheld that probably flew under most people's radar. The Sega Nomad was essentially a portable Sega Genesis. That's right, you could play your favorite Genesis games on the go! Sure, it was a little on the bulky side, but who cares when you can blast through levels of Sonic the Hedgehog and Streets of Rage anywhere you want? The Nomad may not have been the most practical handheld, but it definitely had its moments.

9. TurboExpress:

The TurboExpress was like the prodigal son of handheld gaming. It was the portable version of the TurboGrafx-16, a console that never quite gained the popularity of its competitors. But don't let that fool you! The TurboExpress was a powerhouse of a handheld, with impressive graphics and a library of games that would make any retro gaming enthusiast drool. It may not have been the most well-known handheld, but it definitely deserved more love.

10. Wonderswan Color:

Last but certainly not least, we have the Wonderswan Color. Now, this handheld may not have made much of a splash outside of Japan, but it had its fair share of dedicated fans. With its unique design and an impressive lineup of games, including Final Fantasy and Digimon, the Wonderswan Color was a hidden gem in the world of retro gaming. If you're a true connoisseur of handheld gaming, then this little beauty deserves a spot in your collection.

And there you have it, folks! 10 must-have retro gaming handhelds that will bring back all those warm and fuzzy feelings of nostalgia. Whether you're a die-hard Nintendo fan, a Sega aficionado, or a fan of the lesser-known handhelds, there's something on this list for everyone. So, dust off those cartridges, charge up those batteries, and get ready for a trip back in time. Happy gaming!
