Unleashing Nostalgia: Your Ultimate Guide to the Best Retro Gaming Handhelds in 2023

Ah, retro gaming handhelds, the little pieces of tech that make you feel like you are eight again and your biggest concern was whether to tackle the next boss in Super Mario or save the princess in Zelda. Remember when the biggest problem you had was your game cartridge not working and the magical solution was blowing into it? Good times, right?

Now, we are in 2023. We can create 3D models of ourselves with our phones, have cars that drive us around, and even have a TV series about a murderous, time-traveling banana. (Seriously, who comes up with this stuff?) But for now, let's forget all of these contemporary marvels and step back into an era where 64-bits was the pinnacle of technology.

Why Retro Gaming Handhelds?

You might ask, "Why in the name of Luigi would I play on a device that was born when dinosaurs roamed the earth?" Well, my friend, there are two kinds of people in the world. Those who have experienced the joy of retro gaming, and those who haven't...yet.

Retro gaming isn't just about playing games. It's an experience. It's the feeling of clicking physical buttons, the sound of your game cartridge fitting into the slot, and the satisfaction of watching your character move on a non-HD screen. Plus, they just don't make games like they used to, do they?

The Top Retro Gaming Handhelds of 2023

Alright, alright, enough with the nostalgia. Let's get into the meat and potatoes of this post: the top retro gaming handhelds of 2023. Buckle up, because we're diving headfirst into the past, armed only with AA batteries and a lanyard.

The Evercade

First on our list is the Evercade. This little gem offers a curated list of retro games pre-installed. No need to worry about lost cartridges or searching the depths of the internet for a ROM of Battletoads. Evercade's got you covered.

What's that? You miss the good old days of swapping cartridges? Well, Evercade's got that too! They release new cartridges periodically, so you can keep your collection growing.

The Anbernic RG351P

The Anbernic RG351P, or as I like to call it, "The Anbernic Beasty Boy," is a true beast in the world of retro handhelds. With a Linux-based system, it emulates everything from the original Game Boy to the PlayStation 1.

And it doesn't just play games. Oh no, it plays movies, music, and even e-books. Who knew you could switch from fragging enemies in DOOM to reading 'War and Peace' on the same device?

The GPD XD Plus

The GPD XD Plus is basically the Swiss Army Knife of retro gaming handhelds. This android-based beast can emulate almost any system up to the 5th generation. It even has a clamshell design that makes it look like a love child of the DS and the PSP.

And it's not all about the games. The GPD XD Plus offers the flexibility of Android, meaning you can stream Netflix, browse Reddit, and reply to your grandmotherâ€™s Facebook comments, all in between your gaming sessions.

Wrapping Up

So, there you have it, folks. These little boxes of joy might not have the graphical prowess of an Xbox Series X or the portability of a Nintendo Switch. They might not even be able to run Fortnite (thank goodness). But they bring something more to the table - a feeling of nostalgia and a reminder of simpler times.

So, are you ready to unleash your inner child and dive into the world of retro gaming? Remember, it's not always about the pixels. Sometimes, it's about the memories those pixels bring back.
