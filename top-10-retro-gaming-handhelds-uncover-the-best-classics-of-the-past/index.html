Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Uncover the Best Classics of the Past

Ah, the era of retro handheld gaming. Remember when your biggest concern was whether your Game Boy batteries would last the entire road trip? Or when you'd sneak your favorite handheld under the covers after bedtime for just one more level? Let's take a stroll down memory lane and dig up some of the best classics of the past. Buckle up, it's going to be a pixelated ride!

1. Nintendo Game Boy

We can't talk about retro handheld gaming without paying homage to the king itself: the Nintendo Game Boy. This chunky, grey brick (with a screen as green as your grandma's avocado bathroom suite) was the must-have toy of the 90s. Who could forget the thrill of playing Tetris or Super Mario Land for the first time?

2. Sega Game Gear

Next up, we've got the Sega Game Gear. Remember this guy? Sega's answer to the Game Boy, complete with a color screen (eat your heart out, Nintendo!). It was like carrying a mini Sega Genesis around in your pocket...if you had really big pockets, that is.

3. Atari Lynx

Atari Lynx, anyone? No? Just me? Okay, moving on...

Just kidding! Look, the Lynx was the first handheld with a color screen and it even let you flip the controls for left-handed play. That's innovation right there, folks. Too bad it was about as portable as a toaster oven.

4. Nintendo Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance, or as I like to call it, 'the Game Boy that finally figured out color'. With a sleek design and an impressive game library, this was the handheld that stole countless hours of sleep from kids (and let's be honest, adults) everywhere.

5. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color. Ah, a handheld console for the discerning gamer. It was like the caviar of retro handhelds. With a snazzy click-stick and a host of exclusive games, it definitely made a splash in the handheld scene... or it would have, if more than twelve people had bought one.

6. Nintendo Game Boy Color

Then came the Game Boy Color. It was like the Game Boy Advance's older, less successful sibling. But hey, it brought us Pok√©mon Gold and Silver, so it's alright in my book!

7. TurboExpress

TurboExpress, the handheld version of the TurboGrafx-16 console, was a beast. It ate six AA batteries for breakfast and was about as portable as a cinder block. But hey, it had a TV tuner accessory, so who's laughing now?

8. Sega Nomad

The Sega Nomad, or as I like to call it, the 'why the heck does this thing need six batteries' handheld. It was basically a portable Sega Genesis, which was pretty cool. If only it didn't have the battery life of a mayfly.

9. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS. Two screens? Touch controls? It was like living in the future! And let's not forget the hours spent yelling at Nintendogs or getting lost in The Legend of Zelda: Phantom Hourglass.

10. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

Finally, we've got the PSP. A handheld that proved Sony could do more than just home consoles. With its sleek design and powerful hardware, the PSP was like having a PS2 in your pocket. Just, you know, without the need for a TV and a power outlet.

So there you have it, folks. A nostalgic trip down the pixelated path of retro handheld gaming. These consoles might not hold a candle to the Switch or the PS5, but hey, they're classics for a reason. And whether it was the Game Boy, the Game Gear, or even the Lynx, we all had our favorite. So here's to the handhelds of yesteryears, may you forever live on in our hearts (and in our attics).
