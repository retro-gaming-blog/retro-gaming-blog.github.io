The Ultimate Guide to Retro Gaming Handhelds: Top Models, Hidden Gems, and FAQs Answered

Who needs a PlayStation 5 or an Xbox Series X when you've got 8-bit graphics and a monochrome screen, am I right? Okay, I didn't think so. But, let's take a trip down memory lane with some retro gaming handhelds that still bring a big, nostalgic grin to our faces.

Top Models: Because Old is Gold

Game Boy (1989)

When I think of handheld retro gaming, the first thing that comes to mind is the iconic Game Boy. Released in 1989 (before some of you were even a twinkle in your parents' eyes), the Game Boy was the ultimate companion for long car rides and sneaky under-the-desk gaming sessions during math class. With classics like Tetris and Super Mario Land, it was a pocket-sized portal to pixelated perfection.

Game Gear (1990)

Not to be outdone, SEGA launched the Game Gear in 1990. This handheld had a color screen, you guys. In 1990! While it didn't have the battery life to compete with the Game Boy (seriously, it was like it ran on hamsters on wheels), it had a great library of games and was a worthy competitor.

Atari Lynx (1989)

Let's not forget about Atari Lynx. Now this was a beast! It had a backlit, color screen and even boasted some pretty amazing graphics for its time. But like the Game Gear, it had a serious hunger for batteries.

Hidden Gems: The Unsung Heroes

Neo Geo Pocket (1999)

Ah, the Neo Geo Pocket, the handheld that time forgot. Released by SNK in 1999, it was the underdog of its era, but boy did it pack a punch. Its 16-bit graphics and unique clicky joystick made it a joy to play. And don't even get me started on its legendary fighting games!

Wonderswan (1999)

Here's one for you, the Wonderswan. Never heard of it? I'm not surprised. It was only released in Japan and was the brainchild of the Game Boy's original creator. Its unique feature was the ability to play games both vertically and horizontally. While it didn't make a huge splash globally, it has gained cult status among hardcore retro gamers.

FAQs Answered: Because Knowledge is Power

Can I Still Buy These Handhelds?

Absolutely! Many of these consoles are available on auction sites and retro gaming stores. But be prepared to pay a pretty penny for some of them; they're not just games, they're pieces of history.

Can I Play Modern Games on These?

Now why would you want to do that? Nah, I'm just kidding. The answer is no, though. These retro handhelds were designed to play games from their era. However, with modifications (which I don't necessarily endorse), it might be possible.

Are There Modern Alternatives?

You bet! There are plenty of companies releasing modern versions of these classic handhelds. They come complete with pre-loaded games and a chargeable battery, so you don't have to rob the TV remote for AAs.

Retro gaming handhelds offer us a slice of gaming history. They remind us of a simpler time, when all we needed was a 2-inch screen and a few buttons to keep us entertained for hours. So, next time you think about gaming, go retro, because no matter how shiny and new the PlayStation 5 is, it'll never beat the charm of a good old Game Boy.
