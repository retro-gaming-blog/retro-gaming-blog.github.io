The Ultimate Guide to Retro Gaming Handhelds: Top Models, Hidden Gems, and Answers to Your Burning Questions

Hello, fellow gaming enthusiasts and welcome to your definitive guide to retro gaming handhelds – a nostalgic trip down memory lane, peppered with a dash of humor, a sprinkle of sarcasm, and truckloads of fun! Put on your pixelated glasses, and let's take a journey into the past...

A Time Machine in Your Hands

Did you ever dream of holding a time machine in your hands? No? Just me? Well, in any case, retro gaming handhelds are as close as many of us are going to get. They're not just consoles; they're compact, pocket-sized portals to the past. One moment you're lounging on your couch in 2023, the next, you're back in your childhood living room, engrossed in an intense game of 'Super Mario Land' on your original Gameboy. Ah, bliss!

Top Models: The Cream of the Crop

Let's dive into the deep end, shall we? Here's my round-up of some of the most iconic retro handheld consoles that defined generations.

Game Boy

Remember the good ol’ bulky grey box with its greenish screen and the addictive 'Tetris' cartridge? Yes, the Game Boy! It's the one that started it all. Surely, you remember the thrill of finally defeating Bowser in 'Super Mario Land' after a gazillion attempts, right? No? Oh, come on, don't leave me hanging here!

Game Gear

Up next, we have Sega's Game Gear. With a color screen that could make any Game Boy green with envy, this device was undoubtedly ahead of its time. Battery life? Well, that’s a story for another day. Good luck if you were on a road trip with this bad boy!

Neo Geo Pocket Color

Are you one of those who had the Neo Geo Pocket Color? Congratulations, you're part of an exclusive club! This handheld console was the unsung hero of its era. From 'Sonic' to 'Metal Slug', the gaming titles were impressive. Remember trading gaming cartridges with your best friend over the weekend? Yes, it was the precursor to the modern-day iTunes!

Hidden Gems: Unearthed for Your Pleasure

Like a seasoned archaeologist, I’ve dug deep and unearthed some lesser-known handhelds that deserved a lot more love than they got.

Lynx

Atari's Lynx was a handheld poised to take the world by storm but unfortunately ended up as more of a gentle drizzle. With a color screen and impressive graphics, it was a marvel in its own right. However, it was bigger and heavier than a scientific calculator. Who wants to lug that around?

WonderSwan

Here’s one you might not have heard of unless you're a hardcore retro gaming nerd: the WonderSwan. Brought to life by the legendary Game Boy creator Gunpei Yokoi, this Japanese exclusive handheld contained some real gems. Want to play 'Final Fantasy' on the go in the late 90s? This was your guy!

Burning Questions: Answered!

Here’s where we answer those burning questions that keep you up at night. You’re welcome!

“Are retro handhelds worth collecting?”

Are vinyl records worth collecting? Is vintage clothing worth collecting? Is any old stuff worth collecting? If it brings you joy, nostalgia, or even a smidge of satisfaction - then yes, my friend, retro handhelds are worth collecting.

“Can I still get games for these consoles?”

The good news is - yes, you can pick up games for most of these systems if you look hard enough. The bad news? Prepare to shell out some serious dough for the more sought-after titles!

“Are these handhelds compatible with modern TVs?”

Now, why would you want to do that? Kidding aside, most retro handhelds aren't compatible with modern TVs right out of the box. However, with the right converters and a bit of tech savviness, you can certainly make it work!

And that's a wrap on our journey through the pixelated past of retro gaming handhelds. Whether you're a seasoned collector or a newbie just stepping into the world of retro gaming, I hope you've picked up a nugget or two from this guide. Now, if you'll excuse me, my Game Boy Color is calling my name. Until next time, keep gaming on, friends!
