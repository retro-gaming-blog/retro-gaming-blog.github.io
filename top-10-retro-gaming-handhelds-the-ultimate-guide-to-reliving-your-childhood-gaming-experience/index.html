Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: The Ultimate Guide to Reliving Your Childhood Gaming Experience

Hey there, gaming enthusiasts and nostalgia junkies! Have you ever found yourself reminiscing about those good ol’ days of monochrome screens, pixelated characters and 8-bit music while mashing buttons on your PS5 or Xbox Series X? Are you ready for a deep dive into the golden age of handhelds? If you nodded your head in agreement or even uttered a soft "heck yeah", this blog post is for you!

1. Game Boy

Ah, the Game Boy, the granddaddy of all handhelds. I mean, who hasn't spent countless hours on this little bundle of joy, playing Tetris or Super Mario Land? It was like they injected fun directly into our eyeballs! No backlight? No problem. That's what the lamp next to your bed was for, right?

2. Game Boy Color

Speaking of Game Boys, let's talk about the Game Boy Color. This was Nintendo's way of saying, "Hey! You know all those games you loved in black and white? Let's add some color!" And boy, did they ever. The Game Boy Color brought our favorite games to life in a way we'd never seen before!

3. Game Boy Advance

Then came the Game Boy Advance, the handheld that said, "You thought Game Boy Color was cool? Hold my beer." It was like upgrading from a bicycle to a Ferrari. All of a sudden, we had 32-bit games in our hands! Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire, anyone?

4. PSP (Playstation Portable)

Sony’s PSP was like the cool kid on the block. Remember the first time you saw one of those? You knew there was no going back. It was capable of playing games, movies, music, viewing photos, and even browsing the internet! Who needs a laptop, right?

5. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was like the Swiss Army knife of gaming. Two screens? Check. Touch input? Check. Microphone? Check. The ability to play games from the previous generation? Double-check! It was all the rage, and games like Mario Kart DS and New Super Mario Bros were the cherry on top!

6. Neo Geo Pocket Color

SNK's Neo Geo Pocket Color was like the indie band of handheld consoles. It might not have had the commercial success of other handhelds, but boy, did it have a cult following! With games like Samurai Shodown and Metal Slug 1st Mission, its fans were in for a treat!

7. Sega Game Gear

The Sega Game Gear was like the rebel in the group. It was Sega's answer to the Game Boy, and it came with a color screen and a landscape form factor. Sure, it ate batteries like candy, but who cares when you're playing Sonic on the go?

8. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was like that one friend who was always ahead of the curve. The first handheld with color LCD? Lynx did it. The first with multiplayer capability? Lynx did it. It was seriously underrated but had some great games like Blue Lightning and Chip's Challenge.

9. Nintendo Game & Watch

The Nintendo Game & Watch was like the one that started it all. These were simple LCD games, but they were also timekeeping devices. Hence the name, Game & Watch. Donkey Kong, anyone?

10. WonderSwan

Finally, let's talk about the WonderSwan. It's like the hidden gem of handhelds. Released only in Japan by Bandai, it gave the Game Boy a run for its money. With titles like Final Fantasy and Digimon, it certainly had its moments in the sun!

And there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds to relive your childhood gaming experience. Now, if you’ll excuse me, I've got a high score on Tetris to beat on my Game Boy. Happy gaming!
