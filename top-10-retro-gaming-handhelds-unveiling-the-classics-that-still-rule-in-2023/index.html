Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Classics That Still Rule in 2023

Hello there, gaming nerds and nostalgia enthusiasts! Buckle up, because we're about to take a stroll down memory lane. Remember when cheat codes were a thing? When batteries lasted more than an hour? When you had to blow into game cartridges to make them work? Ah, good times!

Today, we’re diving into the delightful world of retro gaming handhelds. You know them. You love them. And you’ve probably lost countless hours of sleep to them. So let's get into it. Here are the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that still rule the roost in 2023.

1. Game Boy

Ah, the Game Boy. If you're a 90’s kid, you probably had one of these bad boys. Remember when Pokémon Red and Blue graced this small grey box? Those were the days, weren't they? Tetris, Zelda, Super Mario Land - this little gem brought us so much joy and countless hours of entertainment.

2. Game Gear

Sega's answer to the Game Boy had one major thing going for it - color! The Game Gear was like the rich cousin who had all the cool toys while we were stuck with our black and white screens. Sonic on the go was a dream come true for all Sega fans.

3. Neo Geo Pocket

Not as famous as the others on the list, but the Neo Geo Pocket was a powerhouse in its own right. This was like the hipster of handheld gaming - obscure, eccentric, and full of character. With a unique click-stick and some solid games, it’s a real cult classic.

4. Atari Lynx

Atari Lynx was a beefy boy, wasn't it? This thing could drain six AA batteries in four hours. But who cares about battery life when you've got the first color handheld with a backlit screen, right?

5. PlayStation Portable

Why limit yourself to tiny, pixelated characters when you can get fully rendered 3D graphics on the go? The PlayStation Portable was like the hot neighbor who moved in and suddenly made everyone look bad. So much power in such a small package!

6. Nintendo DS

ALERT! We have a two-screen situation here. The Nintendo DS was a fresh approach to handheld gaming. Who doesn't love a good underdog story right? When it first came out, many doubted the dual-screen approach, but look who's laughing now?

7. Sega Nomad

For those of you who are thinking, "What the heck is a Sega Nomad?", it was basically a portable Genesis. Yes, you read that correctly. This was like carrying an entire home console in your backpack. Talk about being ahead of its time!

8. Game Boy Advance

Remember when Game Boy grew up and decided to turn sideways? That's the Game Boy Advance for you. This little number was a significant step up from the original Game Boy, offering better graphics and an impressive game library.

9. WonderSwan

Released only in Japan, this handheld was the brainchild of the OG Game Boy creator. How's that for pedigree, huh? With a long battery life and a varied game library, the WonderSwan is a hidden gem in retro gaming handhelds.

10. Nintendo Game Boy Color

Last but certainly not least, the Game Boy Color. Nintendo took the original Game Boy, slapped on a color screen, and created an instant classic. It was like when your favorite black and white movie gets colorized, but way less controversial.

So there you have it, folks! Our top 10 retro handhelds that still have us wrapped around their little...thumbs? They might not have touchscreens or VR capabilities, but these classics have a special place in our hearts (and our gaming collections). So, why not go rummage through your attic and dust off that old Game Boy? You know you want to.
