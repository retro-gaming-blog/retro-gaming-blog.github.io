Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Must Own: Unleashing Nostalgia in 2023

Hello, fellow gaming geeks, and welcome to the nostalgia train! Choo choo! All aboard!

Remember the days when you'd huddle under your blanket, handheld gaming device in tow, trying to beat that final boss while your parents thought you were asleep? Oh, the thrill! Today, we're going to take a trip down memory lane and revisit those magical devices. Why? Because, why not? It's 2023, and we're all about embracing the past. So, let's dive into the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that you must own.

1. Game Boy

Ah, the Game Boy. Who could forget this gem? Released way back in 1989, this was the handheld that started it all. It was the little grey box that could. It gave us timeless classics like 'Super Mario Land' and 'The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening'. If you don't own one of these, are you even a gamer?

2. Game Boy Color

After the Game Boy came the Game Boy Color in 1998. This device brought us into the world of technicolor. No more monochrome gaming! It was like Dorothy stepping into Oz. If you didn't own one of these, you were probably the kid who was always picked last in P.E.

3. Game Boy Advance

Next on our list is the Game Boy Advance. The GBA, as it's fondly known, was the cool kid on the block in 2001. With its sleek design and advanced (pun intended) features, it was the dream of every kid (and adult, let's be honest).

4. Nintendo DS

In 2004, Nintendo said, "Hey, why have one screen when you can have two?" And thus, the Nintendo DS was born. This device was revolutionary, and it gave us some of the best games of all time. 'Nintendogs', anyone?

5. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Sony entered the handheld market with the PSP in 2004, and boy, did they make an entrance. This device was a powerhouse, with its ability to play movies and music, and of course, some killer games. Plus, it had a joystick. A joystick!

6. Sega Game Gear

The Sega Game Gear might not have been as popular as the Game Boy, but it was still a beast of a handheld. Released in 1990, this device was way ahead of its time, with a color screen and a ton of awesome games.

7. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx is another underrated handheld. Released in 1989, this device was the first handheld with a color screen. Plus, it was ambidextrous. That's right, left-handed gamers, Atari's got your back!

8. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color may not be as well-known as some of the other handhelds on this list, but it's still a must-have for any retro gaming enthusiast. With its clicky joystick and impressive library of games, this device is a hidden gem.

9. Nintendo 3DS

The Nintendo 3DS, released in 2011, brought 3D gaming into our hands. Literally. With its glasses-free 3D technology and dual screens, this device was a game-changer (pun totally intended).

10. PlayStation Vita

Last but not least, we have the PlayStation Vita. This device, released in 2011, was Sony's second foray into the handheld market. With its stunning graphics and impressive lineup of games, the Vita is a must-have for any serious gamer.

So, there you have it, folks! The top 10 retro gaming handhelds you must own in 2023. Now, go out there and get your game on! And remember, in the wise words of a certain Italian plumber, "Let's-a go!"
