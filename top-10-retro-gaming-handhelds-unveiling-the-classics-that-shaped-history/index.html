Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Classics That Shaped History

When we hear the words "gaming handhelds", most of us instantly picture a PSP, a Nintendo Switch, or probably the one device that's glued permanently to your hand, your fancy schmancy smartphone (yes, Candy Crush Saga does count as gaming, folks). But let's rewind a good few decades for the sake of nostalgia. Picture those chunky, plastic-encased, pixel spitting machines from yesteryears. You can almost hear the beeping soundtracks and feel the AA batteries fizzling out too quickly, right?

Now, picking the top 10 retro gaming handhelds is a bit like picking your favorite children. It's tough, someone's going to cry and they're all special in their own way. But let's get to it!

1. Game Boy

Well, duh! If you didn't see this one coming, I don't know what to tell you. This little grey brick, in all its monochromatic glory, was like the Jesus of handheld gaming systems. Released by Nintendo in 1989, it was legendary, revolutionary, and most importantly, it had Tetris.

2. Atari Lynx

Atari Lynx was the cool, older cousin to Game Boy who had color graphics and played California Games. It was bulky, devoured batteries faster than a werewolf under a full moon, but boy, did we love it. Despite its technical prowess, poor Lynx could never quite outshine its Nintendo rival.

3. Game Gear

This shiny black Sega device was like the rebel kid in school who had a cool leather jacket and knew how to skateboard. Launched in 1990, the Game Gear brought color gaming and a backlit screen to our hands, making nights under the bed covers playing Sonic the Hedgehog a little less straining on the eyes.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Neo Geo? More like Neo D'oh! This late 90s handheld allowed you to face off against your friend's team of fighters in the King of Fighters R-2. Also, it had a switchable game language. Japanese Samurai Showdown in Math class? Yes, please!

5. Nintendo Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance was the handheld that brought 32-bit gaming into the palm of our hands. It had a killer line-up of games and a see-through casing reminiscent of those cool 90s telephones. It was like the Beverly Hills 90210 of handhelds â€” cool, colorful, and boasting a ridiculously good line-up.

6. Psion Series 5

Now, this wasn't technically a gaming handheld, more of a PDA. But remember those greyscale arcade conversions of Doom? Plus, it had a keyboard. A KEYBOARD, people!

7. TurboExpress

This one was for the bourgeoisie of the early '90s. The TurboExpress was insanely expensive but it allowed you to play TurboGrafx-16 console games. On a handheld! If you had one of these, you were basically royalty.

8. WonderSwan

Only in Japan, folks, only in Japan. This Bandai piece was affordable, had a great battery life and a plethora of games. It even gave Nintendo a run for its money. But did we ever get it in the West? Nope.

9. Sega Nomad

This was a portable Mega Drive, friends! It was as heavy as a brick and ate batteries like they were going out of style (6 AA batteries for 2 hours), but you could play Streets of Rage while on the bus!

10. Nintendo DS

Before you go, "Wait, isn't DS too contemporary for a retro list?", let me remind you of something scary: The DS was released over 15 years ago. Feeling old yet? Besides, its dual-screen, stylus-using, Wi-Fi-connecting charm is undeniable.

So there you have it, folks! The top 10 retro gaming handhelds that had us sneaking around our homework and playing in the dim glow of a streetlight after bedtime. Those were the good old days, weren't they? But hey, who's to say you can't relive them now? Dust off your old consoles, replace the batteries and let's get back to gaming like it's 1989!

Now, where did I put my Game Boy?
