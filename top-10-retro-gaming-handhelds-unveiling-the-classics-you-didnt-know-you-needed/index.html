Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Classics You Didn't Know You Needed

Ah, the nostalgic journey down the pixelated lane of retro gaming handhelds. Remember when the only thing that mattered was getting to the next level on Tetris? Or the legendary boss battles in 'The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening'? If you're anything like me (a self-proclaimed retro gaming enthusiast), you still dream about these things and occasionally get a hankering for the simpler times. And that's totally okay. After all, who needs reality when you can live in 8-bit heaven? So, buckle up, folks -- we're about to take a stroll down memory lane and revisit the top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds!

1. GAME BOY

Where else could we possibly start but with the iconic Game Boy? This bad boy hit the scene in 1989 and took over the world like Beatlemania (only slightly less screaming). A perfect companion on those long family road trips, Game Boy was the thing that saved most of us from hours of 'I spy' and '20 questions'. Tetris, Super Mario Land, and Pokémon Red and Blue were the staples of our childhood, and Game Boy was the VHS to our favorite movies.

2. THE ATARI LYNX

The Atari Lynx, much like the real lynx, was a bit elusive – and let me tell you, if you owned one of these, you were the absolute bee's knees. As the first handheld with color LCD, it was ahead of its time – like Beethoven composing symphonies while everyone else was playing 'Chopsticks'.

3. GAME GEAR

If you were more into Sega than Nintendo, then the Game Gear was your go-to handheld. With a brightly lit, color screen and a library of games that included the likes of Sonic the Hedgehog, it was a worthy contender in the 90's handheld market. Plus, it had a TV tuner add-on! Watching The Simpsons on a Game Gear at recess? Totally tubular.

4. NEO GEO POCKET

In the land of the giants, the Neo Geo Pocket was the brave underdog. It may not have sold as many units as the Game Boy or Game Gear, but it still packed a punch. It even had a joystick. A joystick, people – on a handheld!

5. NINTENDO DS

The Nintendo DS was the equivalent of having a Siamese twin. Two screens, double the fun, right? Not only did it introduce touchscreen technology (way before we started spending hours catching imaginary monsters on our phones), it was backward compatible. This meant you could play your Game Boy Advance games on it. Talk about a win-win!

6. PLAYSTATION PORTABLE (PSP)

Who could forget the PSP? This sleek piece of kit made every gamer's dream come true: playing PlayStation games on the move. Grand Theft Auto? Check. Tekken? You bet. Just remember, your fingers and thumbs are mortal enemies in the PSP universe.

7. GAME BOY COLOR

Next up is the Game Boy Color – the 90s kid equivalent of upgrading from a black and white TV to color. Suddenly, our favorite games were no longer monochrome, and it was amazing. In many ways, the Game Boy Color was like dessert. You didn’t necessarily NEED it, but boy did it make life sweeter!

8. TURBOEXPRESS

The TurboExpress was essentially a portable version of the TurboGrafx-16 home console. Yes, you read it right. This monstrous machine was playing console games while others were still stuck playing basic handheld games. Borrowing from the wise words of Kevin McCallister, it was the "Rolls Royce of handheld gaming".

9. GAME BOY ADVANCE

The Game Boy Advance was the teenager we all wanted to be – cool, stylish, and full of potential. A veritable feast of incredible games, it took everything we loved about the Game Boy and the Game Boy Color and made it even better. Plus, it could play older Game Boy games, making it a bonafide time machine.

10. NINTENDO SWITCH

Last on our list is the Nintendo Switch. "But that's not retro!" I hear you protesting. Well, it's got a treasure trove of retro games available for download. It's like having your retro cake and eating it, too.

There you have it – the top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds that you didn't know you needed. Sure, modern gaming is great and all – after all, who doesn't love 4K graphics and VR headsets? But where's the fun in playing hyper-realistic games when you can engage in 8-bit boss battles, save pixelated princesses, and collect pixel power-ups? So, step back from reality, dust off your old handhelds, and live a little in the retro gaming world. You know you want to.
