Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unleashing Nostalgia in 2023 - Your Ultimate Guide

Oh, what's that? You thought gaming was all about the latest PS5 or Xbox Series X? Well, my dear friend, it's time to take a trip down memory lane. Today, we're going to be talking about the gaming equivalent of bell-bottoms and lava lamps - retro gaming handhelds. Yes, those pixelated, button-mashing gems that made our childhoods colorful (or monochromatic, depending on when you jumped in). So, buckle up, because we're about to embark on a magical nostalgia trip to the top 10 retro gaming handhelds. Ready? Let's go!

1. The Game Boy

Do I even need to introduce this one? The Game Boy was the handheld that started it all. If you've never played Tetris on the original Game Boy, have you even gamed, bro? The monochrome screen, the beautiful simplicity, the strange joy of blowing into the cartridge to make it work (which, by the way, Nintendo says we shouldn't do, but hey, if it works, it works!). It's like the Beatles of handheld gaming - timeless, revolutionary, and with a fan base that spans generations.

2. Game Boy Color

If the Game Boy was the Beatles, then the Game Boy Color was like Bob Dylan going electric. Color? In my handheld gaming device? It's more likely than you think. Suddenly, Pikachu wasn't just a collection of grey pixels - he was yellow! And Marioâ€™s overalls? They were blue, as they should be! Truly a game-changer.

3. Game Boy Advance

Ah, the Game Boy Advance, or as I like to call it, "my childhood". It brought us some of the best Pokemon games (I'm looking at you, Ruby and Sapphire), a whole bunch of Mario, and even some Zelda. Plus, the design was sleek as heck. It felt like holding the future, only pixelated.

4. Nintendo DS

Remember when we thought two screens was excessive? Yeah, me neither. The Nintendo DS was like the cool older sibling who comes home from college with all these new ideas and suddenly you're questioning everything you thought you knew. Touch screen? Inbuilt microphone? PictoChat? It was a wild ride.

5. PSP

Just when we thought Nintendo had the handheld market all to itself, Sony came in with the PSP. The graphics were slick, it played movies, and you could even surf the web. Nintendo, who?

6. Sega Game Gear

C'mon, did you really think I'd forget about Sega? The Game Gear was like the underdog, always trying to outdo the Game Boy. It had a color screen before the Game Boy, but it never quite managed to win the popularity contest. Still, it deserves a spot on this list for sheer perseverance.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color

This one's for the real connoisseurs. The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the caviar of retro gaming handhelds - a little pricier, a little fancier, and with some genuinely great games. It was like the hipster coffee shop of gaming - not everyone knew about it, but those who did, loved it.

8. Atari Lynx

Okay, so maybe the Lynx was a little ahead of its time (and a little behind on battery life), but it was the first handheld with a color screen, and for that, we salute it. Plus, who didn't love a bit of California Games on the go?

9. Nintendo 3DS

If the DS was the cool older sibling, the 3DS was like the younger sibling trying to outdo them. "Oh, you have two screens? Well, I have 3D!" It was a gimmick, sure, but it was our gimmick. And let's not forget the StreetPass feature that made us go outside for a change.

10. PS Vita

And finally, the PS Vita, Sony's second go at the handheld market. It may not have been as popular as its Nintendo counterparts, but it had its moments. Plus, it had that sweet, sweet dual analog stick setup.

And there you have it, folks - the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that have a special place in our hearts. Each one, a pocket-sized portal to different worlds and adventures. So, next time you're lusting after the latest gaming tech, just remember - there's a whole world of retro gaming waiting for you, and it's only a cartridge blow away.
