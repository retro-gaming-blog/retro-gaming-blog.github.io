Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds of All Time: Your Ultimate Guide

Hello, fellow gaming enthusiasts, and welcome to my world of pixelated wonder! Today, I'm taking you on a journey down memory lane, back to a simpler time when the only battle royale we knew was the mad scramble for batteries to keep our gaming handhelds alive. That's right, folks! We're talking about retro gaming handhelds, the little devices that could (and did!) make a long car ride bearable, and cause plenty of squabbles among siblings.

So, without further ado, let's dive into the top 10 retro gaming handhelds of all time.

1. Game Boy

Oh, the Game Boy, the handheld that started it all. You were as basic as they come, with your monochrome screen and your chunky buttons. But who could forget the countless hours spent on 'Tetris' or 'Super Mario Land'? The Game Boy was the beginning of portable gaming, and oh boy, did it change the game!

2. Game Boy Color

The Game Boy Color! The hip, younger sibling to the original Game Boy, who showed up to the party wearing all the colors of the rainbow. And suddenly, our favorite games, like 'Pok√©mon Red and Blue', didn't seem so... grey. A moment of silence for all the AA batteries this little guy consumed.

3. Game Boy Advance

Is it a bird? Is it a plane? No, it's the Game Boy Advance! Shaped like a spaceship and with a wider screen for your gaming pleasure. The addition of shoulder buttons made us feel like we were holding a little spaceship in our hands, ready to blast off into the world of 'Metroid Fusion'.

4. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS, or as I like to call it, the 'double trouble'. Not one, but two screens? Nintendo really was pushing the boat out with this one. Who knew that the bottom touchscreen would make games like 'Brain Age' so much fun? It was like having a gaming console and a PDA all in one!

5. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

The PlayStation Portable, a.k.a the PSP. Oh, what a time to be alive! Sony's answer to the Nintendo DS, it was sleek, it was sexy, and it could play 'God of War'. Need I say more?

6. Game Gear

Ah, the Game Gear, Sega's attempt at taking down the Game Boy. It may not have won the war, but with its color screen and a TV tuner, it won our hearts. Plus, it was the only way to play 'Sonic the Hedgehog' on the go.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color, the little handheld that could. With its nifty clicky joystick and a solid lineup of games, it was a worthy contender. It might not have been as popular as the others, but it had a charm all its own.

8. Atari Lynx

Atari Lynx, the granddaddy of handheld gaming. It was the first color handheld and the first with a backlit screen. A bit chunky, a bit pricey, but innovative for its time - that was the Lynx.

9. WonderSwan Color

The WonderSwan Color, it's like the unicorn of retro handheld gaming. Only released in Japan, this little gem had some great games and a design that was way ahead of its time. If you've got one stashed away, consider yourself lucky!

10. Sega Nomad

Finally, we come to the Sega Nomad. A handheld that could play your Genesis cartridges! It was like having a mini Genesis in your backpack. Battery life was a joke, but hey, small price to pay for portable 'Sonic', right?

And there you have it, folks! The top 10 retro gaming handhelds of all time. They may be relics of the past now, but they sure left a mark on our gaming hearts. So, which one was your favorite? Or have you been a smartphone gamer all your life, oblivious to the magic of these humble handhelds? Either way, I hope you enjoyed this trip down memory lane!

Until next time, keep gaming and keep those batteries handy!
