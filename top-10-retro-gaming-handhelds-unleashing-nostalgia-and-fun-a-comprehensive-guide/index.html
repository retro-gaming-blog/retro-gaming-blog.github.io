Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unleashing Nostalgia and Fun - A Comprehensive Guide

Alright, folks, buckle up because we're about to take a glorious trip down memory lane. We're talking about the golden age of video games. No, not the era of Fortnite and VR headsets. We're going way back, to a time when handheld gaming consoles were all the rage. Remember when the most pressing issue in your life was trying to beat your high score on Tetris? Ah, those were the days!

1. Game Boy

Let's start with the granddaddy of them all, the Nintendo Game Boy. This bad boy was to the 90s what the iPhone is to the 2023s. Remember the thrill of popping in a new cartridge, blowing into it when it didn't work (because that was obviously the solution), and then spending hours on end trying to catch 'em all in Pokémon Red and Blue? Yeah, me too.

2. Game Boy Color

And then came the Game Boy Color. It was like the Game Boy, but, you know, with color. Revolutionary, right? And who could forget the epic battles in The Legend of Zelda: Oracle of Seasons, or the countless hours spent trying to become a master in Super Mario Bros. Deluxe?

3. Game Boy Advance

Next up, we have the Game Boy Advance. This was the Game Boy on steroids. With a wider screen, more buttons, and even better games, the GBA was a game changer (pun totally intended). It's hard to forget the countless hours spent playing Metroid Fusion or the legendary Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire.

4. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was a true game-changer (I'll stop with the puns now, I promise). With two screens, a touch pen and Wi-Fi capabilities, it was like the handheld console from the future. How many times did you accidentally lose that tiny stylus in the couch though? Just me? Okay, moving on.

5. PSP

Sony's PlayStation Portable, or PSP as it's more commonly known, was the cool kid on the block. With its sleek design, powerful hardware, and extensive library of games, it was hard not to be jealous of the kid who had one. Remember those UMDs? Yeah, those didn't really catch on, did they?

6. Game Gear

The Sega Game Gear was like the Pepsi to Game Boy's Coca-Cola. It was in color, backlit, and had a TV tuner. It was like Sega looked at the Game Boy and said, "Hold my beer." Sure, it ate batteries like no one's business, but hey, that's the price you pay for innovation.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was like that kid in school who was really cool but nobody really knew about. With a joystick instead of a D-pad and some really awesome games, it was a hidden gem in the world of handheld gaming.

8. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was the first handheld console with color graphics. Yes, you heard that right. It was a pioneer in the handheld gaming world, but unfortunately, like many pioneers, it was a bit before its time.

9. TurboExpress

The TurboExpress was essentially a portable version of the TurboGrafx-16 home console. Sounds amazing, right? Well, it was, if you could afford it. With a hefty price tag, it was more of a status symbol than a gaming console. But boy, was it cool.

10. WonderSwan

Last but not least, we have the WonderSwan. This little-known handheld console from Japan had some really unique features, like the ability to be played vertically or horizontally. It might not have been a big hit in the west, but it definitely deserves a spot on this list.

Well, there you have it, folks. A trip down memory lane with the top 10 retro gaming handhelds. So, which one was your favorite? And how many of them did you own? Don't lie, we all know you had at least three. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have some old high scores to beat.
