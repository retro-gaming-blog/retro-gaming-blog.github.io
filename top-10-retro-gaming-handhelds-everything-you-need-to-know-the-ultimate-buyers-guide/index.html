Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Everything You Need to Know - The Ultimate Buyer's Guide

Hey there, gamers! I see you're all about that old school vibe. You adore those pixelated graphics, cherish those tinny soundtracks, and love that feeling of holding a compact piece of gaming history in the palms of your hands, right? Well, let's embark on a journey down memory lane to explore the top 10 retro gaming handhelds. Trust me, by the time we're done, you'll be itching to get your hands on them (again).

1. Game Boy: The OG

Let's start with a device that needs no introduction. Enter the Game Boy, the uncrowned king of the retro gaming realm. This gray brick ushered in a new era of gaming where your annoying siblings couldn't hog the TV. But do you remember the struggle of playing it in the dark? Ah, those were the times!

2. Game Boy Color: The Colorful Successor

Stepping up the game (pun intended) was our next contender, the Game Boy Color. This device was like the Game Boy, but with COLORS! Groundbreaking, right? Sure, those colors looked like they'd been washed a few times too many, but hey, it was a step up from monochrome!

3. Game Boy Advance: The Cool Kid

Next up, we have the Game Boy Advance, the epitome of cool in the early 2000s. Sleek, stylish, and powerful, this bad boy had a horizontal design and a color screen that actually displayed more than five colors. A treat for sore eyes, indeed!

4. Sega Game Gear: The Worthy Rival

Let's give some love to the Game Boy's arch-nemesis, the Sega Game Gear. This beast came with a backlit color screen, something Nintendo couldn't dream of at that time. But remember those six AA batteries it guzzled in three hours? Good times!

5. Nintendo DS: The Dual-Dispenser

The Nintendo DS was a game-changer with its two screens and touch controls. I mean, who needs buttons when you have a stylus, right? Its backward compatibility with Game Boy Advance games was just the cherry on top.

6. PlayStation Portable (PSP): The Multimedia Marvel

Sony's PSP was a handheld that doubled as a media player. Games, music, movies – the PSP did it all. Plus, it had a joystick, finally answering our prayers for precision control.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color: The Underdog

This gem didn't get the recognition it deserved, but the Neo Geo Pocket Color had a charm of its own. Its micro-switch joystick offered a satisfying click every time you moved it. Truly satisfying!

8. Atari Lynx: The Forward-Thinking Feline

With its ambidextrous design and color screen, the Atari Lynx was ahead of its time. Too bad it was the size of a small car! Just kidding, but you could certainly use it for bicep curls.

9. Game & Watch: The Multitasker

Nintendo's Game & Watch were the smartphones before smartphones existed – a game console and a clock in one device? Revolutionary! Plus, who can forget the iconic 'Ball' game?

10. TurboExpress: The Big Spender's Best Friend

The TurboExpress was a handheld version of the TurboGrafx-16 console. It was awesome, high-tech, and cost an arm and a leg. But hey, who needs two kidneys, right?

So, there you have it, folks – the top 10 retro gaming handhelds to remind you of the golden age of portable gaming. Whether you're a nostalgia junkie or a vintage collector, these consoles hold a special place in our hearts and our history. So, which one of these handhelds are you gonna try to score off eBay next?
