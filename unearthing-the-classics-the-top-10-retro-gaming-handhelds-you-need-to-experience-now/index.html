Unearthing the Classics: The Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Need to Experience Now

Oh, hello there! You've stumbled into our little corner of the internet where we fondly look back at the golden age of handheld gaming. No, we're not talking about your fancy Nintendo Switches or PlayStation Vitas. We're going back, way back, to the time when kids played on small screens with pixelated graphics, and they loved it. Don't believe me? Stick around and let's delve into the top 10 retro gaming handhelds you need to experience now.

1. Game Boy

Let's kick things off with the granddaddy of them all, the Nintendo Game Boy. Ah, the memories of blowing into the cartridge slot when the game doesn't load. Who needs a warranty when you have your own breath, right? The Game Boy brought classics like Super Mario Land and The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening into our pockets.

2. Game Boy Color

Just when we thought it doesn't get any better, Nintendo slapped some color into their Game Boy and voila, the Game Boy Color was born! Remember when we thought Pok√©mon Yellow was the height of visual innovation? Good times.

3. Game Boy Advance

Alright, last Game Boy on the list, I promise. The Game Boy Advance, or GBA if you're part of the cool kids club, brought us a horizontal design and shoulder buttons. Not to mention some of the best RPGs ever, like Golden Sun and Final Fantasy Tactics Advance.

4. Nintendo DS

Okay, I lied about the Game Boy thing. But the Nintendo DS is in a league of its own. Two screens? A touch pen? This thing was more high tech than my first cell phone! And let's not forget the magic of pictochat.

5. Sega Game Gear

Moving away from Nintendo, let's give some love to Sega's Game Gear. This little beast was color before it was cool. But let's face it, the best thing about Game Gear was that it had Sonic games. Nothing beats running around at high speed collecting rings, right?

6. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx, a handheld that was way ahead of its time. This baby was the first with color display and the ability to connect with up to 17 other units for multiplayer. Pretty groundbreaking, unless you only had one friend like me.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was like the hipster of retro handhelds. It wasn't as mainstream, but it had some killer games. Ever played SNK vs. Capcom: The Match of the Millennium? If not, you're missing out.

8. PlayStation Portable

Ah, the PlayStation Portable, or PSP as us cool kids call it. This was the handheld for the grown-ups, with its sleek design and games like God of War: Chains of Olympus. Plus, it played movies. Who needs a DVD player when you have a PSP?

9. Nintendo Game & Watch

Before all the Game Boys and DS, there was the Game & Watch. These little handhelds only played one game each, but boy, were they addictive. Who knew juggling balls or catching eggs could be so fun?

10. Bandai Wonderswan

Finally, we have the Bandai Wonderswan. This little-known handheld from Japan had some amazing games if you were willing to navigate through the Japanese language. Plus, it was designed by the same guy who made the Game Boy. Talk about pedigree!

Well, there you have it, our top 10 retro gaming handhelds you need to experience now. Sure, they don't have the fancy graphics of today's consoles, but they have something far more important: charm. So why not take a break from your Fortnite and give these classics a try? Trust me, you won't regret it.
