Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Rediscover the Magic of Vintage Gaming in 2023

Ah, 2023. Where cars don't fly and we still haven't made contact with aliens. But hey, at least we've got some kick-butt retro gaming handhelds, right? So, today, we're going to take a little stroll down memory lane and have a look at some of the best retro gaming handhelds. Why? Because nostalgia is a dish best served pixelated.

1. The Game Boy

Let's start with the OG of handheld gaming, the Nintendo Game Boy. This little grey brick with its pea soup green screen was the portable gaming revolution of the 90s. Tetris, anyone? I mean, who needs color when you can rotate falling blocks with such finesse?

2. The Game Boy Color

Ah, the Game Boy Color, the Game Boy's younger, more vibrant sibling. Suddenly, Pokemon weren't just different shapes, they were different colors too. Mind = blown.

3. The Game Boy Advance

Three cheers for the Game Boy Advance! This was the Game Boy's cooler, older cousin who showed up to family gatherings with the latest games and a backlight. No more squinting in the dark or stealing your mom's reading light.

4. The Sega Game Gear

Alright, let's give Nintendo a break. Remember the Sega Game Gear? This beast ran through batteries like nobody's business, but hey, it had a color screen and Sonic the Hedgehog. Totally worth it.

5. The Neo Geo Pocket Color

This one's a bit of an oddity. Not many people had a Neo Geo Pocket Color, but those who did, loved it. It had a thumbstick instead of a D-pad. A thumbstick! On a handheld! And let's not forget about the epic Samurai Shodown showdowns.

6. The Atari Lynx

Let's hop in our time machines and head back to the 80s. The Atari Lynx was quite the hefty lad, but it was the first handheld with a color screen. Too bad it couldn't save Atari from its fate. Oh well, better luck next time.

7. The PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Yes, we're counting this as a retro handheld because it's over a decade old. Feel old yet? The PSP was like a portable PlayStation 2. You could even watch movies on it. Who needs Netflix when you've got UMDs?

8. The Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was a game-changer, literally. Two screens? Touch functionality? PictoChat? Revolutionary. Plus, it gave us the glory that is Nintendogs. Who knew virtual pet care could be so addictive?

9. The Game & Watch

Before the Game Boy, there was the Game & Watch. These little handhelds each had a single game on them, but boy were they fun. Don't you miss the days when you could play a game, check the time, and set an alarm all on the same device? Yeah, me neither.

10. The Nintendo Switch Lite

Wait, does this count as a retro handheld? Well, it does play retro games so... I'm counting it. The Nintendo Switch Lite is the culmination of all the handheld gaming advances before it. It's like the grandkid that finally makes the family proud.

So there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds of 2023. Now, if you'll excuse me, I'm off to beat my high score on Tetris.
