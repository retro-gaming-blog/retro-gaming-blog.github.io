Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds of All Time: A Comprehensive Guide to Your Nostalgic Quest

Picture this: you're a kid again, playing your favorite game on your trusty handheld, utterly engrossed in a world of pixels and 8-bit music. No, this isn't some strange time travel trick. This is the magic of retro gaming handhelds, my friends. So, buckle up, retro gaming enthusiasts, and let's take a trip down memory lane. Here’s my definitive, totally unscientific, somewhat subjective list of the top 10 retro gaming handhelds of all time.

1. Game Boy/ Game Boy Color

Oh, come on, you knew this was coming. What list of handheld gaming devices would be complete without the Game Boy? Like the Beatles of the gaming world, this little beast revolutionized the industry. And when its colorful cousin, the Game Boy Color, came around, well, let's just say some minds were blown. Who needs shades of grey when you can have the entire rainbow? Answer: nobody.

2. Game Boy Advance/ Game Boy Advance SP

Have you ever tried to play your Game Boy under your blanket with a flashlight? Yeah, me too. Enter the Game Boy Advance with its wider screen and more powerful engine. But the real MVP was the Game Boy Advance SP. Remember when you first flipped open that backlit screen? That's the stuff dreams are made of.

3. Nintendo DS/ DS Lite

The Nintendo DS - two screens, one handheld. It was like the gaming gods finally answered our prayers. And with the DS Lite? Even lighter, even brighter. Who said you can't have it all?

4. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

The PSP was like that cool kid in school who was good at everything. It played games, movies, music, and could even go online. It was like a miniature PlayStation you could fit in your pocket. Now, that's magic.

5. Sega Game Gear

Ah, the Sega Game Gear, the battery eater. This beast of a handheld gave us color graphics and a backlight way before it was cool. Sure, it ate through batteries like a gamer through a bag of Doritos, but who cares when Sonic is in full color?

6. Atari Lynx

Can we have a moment of silence for the underappreciated Atari Lynx? This handheld was truly ahead of its time with color graphics and a backlight. But its biggest claim to fame? It was ambidextrous. That’s right, lefties and righties could play in harmony. It's the handheld we deserved, but not the one we needed at the time.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Can you say, "Ahead of its time?" The Neo Geo Pocket Color could. This handheld had a joystick and was sporting 16-bit graphics while everyone else was still playing with 8-bits. It wasn't just a handheld, it was a statement.

8. Nintendo Game & Watch

The Nintendo Game & Watch was the OG of gaming handhelds. These babies were simple, charming, and addictive. And they also told the time. Who says you can't mix business with pleasure?

9. WonderSwan Color

The WonderSwan Color was like the hipster of handhelds. It was cool, quirky, and not many people had heard of it. With a killer design and some great games, this was a handheld that marched to the beat of its own drum.

10. TurboExpress

And finally, let's not forget the TurboExpress, the handheld that thought it was a home console. It played the same games as its big brother, the TurboGrafx-16. It was like having a home console in your pocket. And in the early '90s? That was mind-blowing.

There you have it, folks – the top 10 retro gaming handhelds of all time. Each one a testament to the evolution of portable gaming, from humble black-and-white beginnings to the colorful, multifunctional devices of today. And let's not forget the countless batteries sacrificed in the name of gaming glory. They were the real heroes here.

So, what's your favorite retro gaming handheld? Are you a Game Boy purist or do PSP graphics get your heart racing? Or maybe, just maybe, you're that one Atari Lynx fan that's been waiting for it to make a comeback.

Whatever your preference, these handhelds are all worthy of a spot in the retro gaming hall of fame. Because let's face it, without them, those long car rides as a kid would have been a lot more boring.
