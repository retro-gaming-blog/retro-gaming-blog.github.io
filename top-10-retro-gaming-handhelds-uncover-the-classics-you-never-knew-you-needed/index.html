Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Uncover the Classics You Never Knew You Needed

Well, hello there, you nostalgic gamers, you. I see you've stumbled upon my little corner of the internet seeking some good old classic gaming action. Don't worry, you're in the right place. Let me guess, the latest gaming consoles with their lifelike graphics and immersive experiences just aren't cutting it for you? Need a simpler time when all you needed was two buttons and a d-pad to save the world? Well, you've come to the right place. Let's dive into the top 10 retro gaming handhelds you never knew you needed (and probably still don't, but hey, who am I to judge?)

1. Game Boy

Did you think I was going to start this list with anything else? Oh, come on now! The Game Boy is the king, the alpha, the OG of handheld gaming. This green-screened beauty gave us classics like Tetris, Pokemon Red and Blue, and The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening. And let's not forget those four AA batteries you had to change every few hours. Ah, the memories.

2. Game Gear

Alright, Sega fans, your time has come. The Game Gear was Sega's answer to the Game Boy, but with a full-color screen. Yes, you heard it right; we had color in the 90s. Game Gear boasted classics like Sonic the Hedgehog and Streets of Rage. Never mind the fact that it needed six AA batteries that barely lasted three hours. Hey, no one said being a retro gamer was easy, or cheap!

3. Atari Lynx

Atari? On a handheld? You betcha. The Atari Lynx was the first handheld console with color graphics. Take that, Game Boy! Sure, it was about the size of a small car, but who cares when you're playing classics like California Games and Chip's Challenge?

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

SNK's Neo Geo Pocket Color was the underdog in the handheld console wars. It might not have had the extensive library of games that the Game Boy had, but it had some quality titles. Games like Samurai Shodown and Metal Slug 1st Mission made it a worthy contender. Plus, it only needed two AA batteries. Take notes, Game Gear.

5. Nintendo DS

Okay, okay, I know what you're thinking. "But this isn't retro, it's not even 20 years old!" Well, dear reader, let me remind you that the original DS was released in 2004. Feel old yet? The DS brought us touch screen gaming and classics like New Super Mario Bros, Brain Age, and Animal Crossing: Wild World.

6. Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance was the Game Boy's cooler, more advanced little brother. With a wider screen and shoulder buttons, it brought us Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire, Metroid Fusion, and The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap. Plus, who can forget the struggle of trying to play it in the dark before the SP model came out with a backlight?

7. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

Sony's first foray into the handheld market was a hit. The PSP was like having a PS2 in your pocket. Games like God of War: Chains of Olympus and Grand Theft Auto: Liberty City Stories made it a must-have for any gamer on the go. Just don't mention the UMDs, those things were a nightmare.

8. Sega Nomad

Ever wanted to take your Sega Genesis games on the go? No? Well, Sega thought you did, and thus the Nomad was born. This beast was essentially a portable Genesis, allowing you to play your favorite titles wherever you were. It was a great idea, in theory, but those six AA batteries it required didn't last long.

9. Nintendo Game & Watch

The granddaddy of them all, the Nintendo Game & Watch was the first gaming handheld. It only played one game, but hey, it also told the time! Who needs a smartphone when you've got this bad boy in your pocket?

10. Wonderswan

Last but not least, we have the Wonderswan. This little-known handheld from Bandai was only released in Japan, but it had some great games. The Final Fantasy series and Digimon games were among its most popular titles.

So there you have it, folks, the top 10 retro gaming handhelds you never knew you needed. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have some Tetris to play on my Game Boy. Happy gaming!
