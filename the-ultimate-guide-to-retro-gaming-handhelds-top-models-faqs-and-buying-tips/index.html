The Ultimate Guide to Retro Gaming Handhelds: Top Models, FAQs, and Buying Tips

Hello, retro gaming enthusiasts! You know who you are. You're the ones who remember blowing into a cartridge to make it work. The ones who know the sweet music of a 16-bit soundtrack. The ones who swear that pixelated graphics are an artistic style, not a limitation. This one's for you.

Top Models

1. Game Boy

First up, let's give a big round of applause to the granddaddy of them all, the Game Boy. This is the little block of plastic that started it all. Released by Nintendo in 1989, the Game Boy was a revelation. Sure, by today's standards, its green and black screen looks more like a calculator than a gaming device, but back in the day, it was the height of technology.

I mean, who could forget the first time they caught a Pikachu in Pokémon Red and Blue, or the countless hours spent playing Tetris on that tiny screen?

2. Sega Game Gear

Next up, we have the Sega Game Gear. Released in 1990, this was Sega's answer to the Game Boy, and it came with a color screen! If you were lucky enough to own one of these, you were the envy of every kid on the school bus.

Sure, it ate batteries like Pac-Man eats pellets, but that was a small price to pay for the ability to play Sonic the Hedgehog in full color on the go.

3. Atari Lynx

Let's not forget the Atari Lynx. This was the first handheld console with a color LCD and it even had a backlit screen! I mean, playing games in the dark? That's some next-level stuff right there.

However, it was also about the size of a small car, so you needed a backpack just to carry it around. But hey, it was the '90s, we all had huge backpacks anyway, right?

FAQs

Now, let's answer some frequently asked questions about retro gaming handhelds.

Why are retro gaming handhelds so popular?

Because they're awesome, that's why! But seriously, nostalgia is a powerful thing. These devices take us back to a simpler time, a time when the most important thing in life was getting to the next level or beating our high score.

Is it worth buying a retro gaming handheld in 2023?

Well, that all depends on what you're looking for. If you want to play the latest AAA titles with the best graphics, then no, a retro gaming handheld is not for you.

But if you want a piece of gaming history, a throwback to your childhood, and a device that's just plain fun, then yes, a retro gaming handheld is absolutely worth it.

Buying Tips

And finally, some tips for buying a retro gaming handheld.

Check the condition

These devices are old. Like, "back when the internet made that weird screeching noise" old. So, make sure you check the condition before you buy. Are the buttons responsive? Does the screen work? Does it smell like it's been stored in a damp basement for 20 years? These are all important questions to ask.

Ask about the battery life

Remember what I said about the Game Gear eating batteries? Well, it's not the only one. Battery technology has come a long way since the '90s, so don't expect these devices to last all day on a single charge.

Don't be afraid to haggle

These are second-hand devices, so don't be afraid to haggle on the price. The worst they can say is no, right?

Well, that's it for now, folks. So, whether you're a seasoned collector or a newbie just getting into the world of retro gaming, I hope you found this guide helpful. Now go out there and get your game on!
