Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Uncover the Best Classics Everyone is Asking About

Oh, the good old days. When you had to blow the dust out of your cartridge to make it work, and the only cheat code you knew was UP, UP, DOWN, DOWN, LEFT, RIGHT, LEFT, RIGHT, B, A. We're talking about retro handheld gaming consoles, my friends, the ultimate blast from the past. So, sit back, grab a cuppa joe, and let's dive headfirst into this pixelated pool of nostalgia.

1. Game Boy

If I have to explain what a Game Boy is, I'm just... I don't even know, man. This little brick of joy is the granddaddy of handheld gaming. Released in 1989 by Nintendo, the Game Boy revolutionized mobile gaming and introduced legendary franchises like Pokémon and Zelda to the world. Still own one? You're a legend.

2. Game Gear

But what about color?, I hear you cry. Enter the Sega Game Gear. Released in 1990, it was in direct competition with the Game Boy. It boasted a full-color backlit display that made Sonic the Hedgehog look like a psychedelic dream.

3. Atari Lynx

"No list of retro gaming handhelds would be complete without the Atari Lynx." Said no one, ever. But, hey, we're about the underdogs here too! The Lynx was the first handheld console with a color LCD, but thanks to its bulky design and short battery life, it never really took off. Still, it has a certain 90s charm to it.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Remember the Neo Geo? Probably not. But that's okay. It was a handheld console by SNK that only had about 10 games. But man, were those games good. The Pocket Color version was a sleek, sexy machine that was way ahead of its time.

5. Game Boy Advance

Back to the Game Boy family, and it's the Advance. This was the handheld that really brought console-quality games to the palm of your hand. With titles like 'Advance Wars' and 'Final Fantasy Tactics', the GBA was a force to be reckoned with.

6. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

If the Game Boy Advance was a force, the PSP was a darn tsunami. This sleek handheld brought PlayStation-quality games to your pocket and even had a rather good movie player built-in. God of War in your pocket? Yes, please.

7. Nintendo DS

I bet you saw this coming. The Nintendo DS burst onto the scene in 2004 with its dual-screen tech and touchscreen controls. Not to mention it was backwards compatible with GBA games. Pretty neat, huh?

8. Game Boy Color

Ah, the Game Boy Color, the younger, more hip cousin of the original Game Boy. It started the trend of gaming handhelds in different colors and introduced many childhood favorites like 'Pokémon Gold and Silver'.

9. Sega Nomad

The Sega Nomad was basically a portable Sega Genesis, which sounds amazing. And it was, except for the fact that it ate batteries for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Still, it gets a spot on our list for ambition alone.

10. WonderSwan

What's that? Never heard of the WonderSwan? That's because it was only released in Japan. But this little beauty had some great games and even a version made in collaboration with Final Fantasy creator Square. Truly a hidden gem.

So there it is folks, the top 10 retro gaming handhelds. Whether you're a nostalgia fiend, a retro collector or just someone who appreciates a good old-fashioned button mashing session, these handheld consoles are where it's at. End of story. Signing off - your fellow gaming geek!
