Unboxing the Past: Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Must Own - A Comprehensive Review and Buying Guide

Hey there, gaming enthusiasts! Ready to take a walk down memory lane? Strap in because we're about to take a deep dive into the world of retro gaming handhelds. You know, the ones that gave you thumb cramps and nearly blinded you with their lack of backlighting? Ah, the good old days. Let's get started, shall we?

1. Game Boy

First on our list, the grandaddy of all handhelds, the Nintendo Game Boy. Released by Nintendo in 1989, this little gray brick was the stuff of dreams. Who could forget the iconic "Tetris" theme? Or the joy of catching your first Pok√©mon? Sure, the 8-bit graphics were about as advanced as a potato, but hey, who needs fancy graphics when you've got charm?

2. Game Boy Color

Next up, say hello to the Game Boy Color. This was Nintendo basically saying: "Hey, remember that Game Boy you love? Here it is again, but now it's got colors!" Revolutionary, right? Still, the addition of color was a game changer (pun intended). Plus, it came in pretty colors like purple and teal. Who could resist?

3. Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance is up next. It's like if the Game Boy hit puberty and came out the other side looking sleek and cool. With its horizontal design and enhanced graphics, it was a massive step up. It also introduced us to some killer games like "Advance Wars". Remember when turn-based strategy games were cool? Me neither, but this game was super fun anyway.

4. Game Gear

Hey, remember Sega? They made games too! The Sega Game Gear was their answer to the Game Boy, but with a full-color screen, no less. It was like a portable Genesis! The downside? It devoured batteries faster than Pac-Man chomping on power pellets.

5. Atari Lynx

Next on our nostalgia trip is the Atari Lynx. This handheld was ahead of its time with features like a backlit color screen and the ability to flip the controls for left-handed players. It was like the nerdy kid in school who was cool before it was cool to be cool. Too bad it was also about as popular.

6. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Moving on, we have the Neo Geo Pocket Color. It was SNK's attempt to take on the Game Boy Color. While it didn't quite succeed, it still had some pretty awesome games. Plus, it had a clicky joystick. Clicky joysticks are cool, aren't they?

7. WonderSwan Color

Ever heard of the WonderSwan Color? No? Well, it was a thing. Released only in Japan by Bandai, it had some amazing titles like "Final Fantasy". It's definitely worth checking out if you can find one. Or if you can read Japanese. Or both.

8. TurboExpress

The TurboExpress was basically a portable TurboGrafx-16. It even played the same game cards! The downside? It cost about as much as a small car. But hey, who needs a car when you can play "Bonk's Adventure" on the go?

9. PSP

Sony's PlayStation Portable, or PSP as it's commonly known, was a powerhouse in its day. It played games, movies, music, and you could even browse the internet. It was like the Swiss Army knife of handhelds. The downside? It was about as pocket-friendly as a brick.

10. Nintendo DS

Last but not least, we have the Nintendo DS. With its dual screens, touchscreen functionality, and a library of games to die for, it's no wonder it's one of the best-selling handhelds of all time. Remember "Brain Age"? No? Just me then.

So, there you have it, folks! Our top 10 retro gaming handhelds. Each of them holds a special place in our hearts and our gaming history. They might not have the flashy graphics or online capabilities of today's handhelds, but they've got something far more important - charm. So why not dust off that old Game Boy and relive the glory days? After all, they don't make 'em like they used to.
