Uncovering the Nostalgia: The Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Must Have in 2023

Ah, retro gaming handhelds. Just saying those words conjures up nostalgia for a simpler, pixelated time when you could carry your favorite games in your pocket and play them whenever and wherever you wanted. Remember those days? Of course you do, you old-timer!

Now, some of you youngsters might be asking: "What's the big deal about these clunky, old devices?" Well, let me tell you, kiddos, these weren't just handhelds, they were our trusty companions, our allies in the eternal struggle against boredom.

So join me on a trip down memory lane as we uncover the 10 retro gaming handhelds that you absolutely must have in 2023, whether you're a seasoned retro gamer looking to relive your childhood or a newbie looking to discover the roots of gaming. Let's dive in, shall we?

1. Game Boy Advance SP

Ah, the Game Boy Advance SP. This little gem from Nintendo was the envy of every kid on the playground back in the day. With its backlit screen and clamshell design, it was like having a pocket-sized NES. Plus, it was backwards compatible with all your Game Boy and Game Boy Color games. What's not to love?

2. Sega Game Gear

The Sega Game Gear was like the rebellious cousin of the Game Boy. It had a color screen and a landscape orientation that set it apart from its monochrome competitor. Sure, it guzzled batteries like a Hummer, but that was a small sacrifice for portable Sonic, right?

3. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was a bit of an oddball, and we loved it for that. It was the first handheld with a color LCD and hardware that could handle 3D graphics. Sure, it was as huge as a brick, but that just meant you got a free workout while gaming.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the David to the Game Boy's Goliath. With a joystick that clicked and a selection of SNK fighting games, this underdog of the gaming world still has a devoted following to this day.

5. Nintendo DS

Nintendo strikes again with the DS, the first handheld with a dual-screen (hence the name). With a touch screen and a microphone, it redefined the portable gaming experience. Remember getting strange looks for blowing into your DS to play Mario Kart? Good times.

6. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

Sony's foray into the handheld market, the PSP was a powerful device that brought console-quality games to your pocket. It also played movies and music, making it one of the first multimedia gaming devices. Just don't mention the UMDs, okay?

7. Game Boy Color

Before there was the Advance, there was the Color. The Game Boy Color brought, well, color to the Game Boy lineup. Plus, Pok√©mon in color. Need I say more?

8. WonderSwan Color

The WonderSwan Color was another underdog in the handheld market. Released only in Japan, it nevertheless has a cult following worldwide. Its unique design allowed for both portrait and landscape gaming. Plus, Final Fantasy. 'Nuff said.

9. Nintendo Game Boy

The one that started it all. The original Game Boy was a game-changer (pun intended) with its monochrome screen and simple controls. Tetris on the Game Boy is still one of the greatest gaming experiences of all time. Fight me.

10. Nintendo 3DS

Last, but not least, the 3DS. The successor to the DS, it brought 3D gaming to the handheld market. Plus, it's backwards compatible with DS games, so you can replay your favorites in glorious 3D.

So there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds you must have in 2023. Whether you're an old-timer looking to relive the good old days or a young whippersnapper looking to discover the classics, these handhelds are sure to delight. Now go out there and get your game on!
