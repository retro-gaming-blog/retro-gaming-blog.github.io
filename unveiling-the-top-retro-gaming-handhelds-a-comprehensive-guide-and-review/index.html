Unveiling the Top Retro Gaming Handhelds: A Comprehensive Guide and Review

Oh, come on. Who among us has not been fond of video games at some point in our lives? Whether you are a 90s kid who grew up on a strict diet of Sonic and Mario, or a millennial who can't help but feel the charm of the pixelated classics, there is a certain allure to retro games that just can’t be denied. It’s like that old, comfy sweater you can’t seem to throw away, isn't it? Well, that's where retro gaming handhelds come in! No, not your fancy-shmancy Nintendo Switch. We're taking it back, way back. So buckle up as we dive into the realm of nostalgia that are retro handheld gaming consoles!

The Nintendo Game Boy - The O.G. (Original Gamer)

Let's kick things off with the granddaddy of handheld consoles— the Nintendo Game Boy. Ah, the good old brick. Chunky, grey, and with a screen so green it could've been a vegetable.

Sure, it only had a 4 shades of green on a tiny screen, but boy oh boy, did we love it! The Game Boy was packed with classics like Tetris, Super Mario Land, and Pokémon Red and Blue. And who can forget the countless hours spent playing, only to have the batteries die at the most crucial moment? (Ah, those were the days).

The Game Boy Color - More than Fifty Shades of Fun!

Then came the Game Boy Color, the cooler, younger sibling of the original. It was sleeker, lighter, and as the name suggests... drumroll... it had color!

The Game Boy Color brought a rainbow into our lives (or at least 56 colors to the screen) and made games like The Legend of Zelda: Oracle of Seasons and Pokémon Crystal a visual treat. It was a big step up, and we couldn't have been happier. Well, unless it came with free pizza... but we can't have everything, can we?

The Sega Game Gear - Sonic's Portable Playground

And now, for the real competitor to the Game Boy, the Sega Game Gear. "But what’s that?" I hear you ask. Well, the Game Gear was Sega's answer to Nintendo's handheld throne. And it wasn't a bad answer, either.

It had a backlit color screen and could even play Master System games with an adapter. And of course, it was the home of our speedy blue hedgehog friend, Sonic. The Game Gear was definitely ahead of its time, and it's still a solid choice for retro gaming on the go. That is if you can handle lugging around 6 AA batteries. Hey, no pain, no game, right?

The Atari Lynx - A Wild Card

Who says it’s all about Nintendo and Sega? Let's not forget the Atari Lynx. This beast was the world's first handheld with a color screen. It was backlit, could be used by both lefties and righties, and even had network gaming. Sounds like a dream, doesn't it?

But, here's the kicker: it was as big as a spaceship and ate batteries like Pac-Man eats dots. But hey, if you want to break a sweat while gaming (and not from the boss fights), the Lynx is your go-to guy.

The Neo Geo Pocket Color - The Underdog

Finally, there's the Neo Geo Pocket Color. Ah, the underdog of the retro handhelds. This little guy came out when the Game Boy Color was all the rage, and boy, did it have some big shoes to fill.

Known for its amazing fighting games like Samurai Shodown and King of Fighters R-2, it won over gamers with its joystick control and excellent battery life. It may not have been a commercial success, but it won the hearts of those who gave it a chance. And isn't that what counts?

There you have it, my fellow gamers. A brief stroll down memory lane and an in-depth look at the top retro gaming handhelds. Each had their pros and cons, and they all brought something unique to the table. The question is, which one was your favorite? Or maybe still is? Ah, the eternal debate. At the end of the day, though, it's not about pixel resolution or battery life. It's about the joy these devices brought us, and the timeless memories they've gifted us. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have a date with a Game Boy and a Tetris high score that won't beat itself. Game on!
