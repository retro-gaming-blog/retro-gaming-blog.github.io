Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Your Ultimate Guide to Nostalgic Gaming in 2023

Ah, retro gaming. It's like stepping into a time machine and being transported back to the era of pixelated graphics, 8-bit soundtracks, and the blissful simplicity of a time before in-app purchases and endless DLCs. Ain't no microtransactions here, folks! But wait, you say, "I want to game on the go, dive down memory lane while I'm stuck on a boring train ride". Fear not, my pixel-loving friends! We're here to talk about the best retro gaming handhelds that 2023 has to offer.

1. Game Boy Advance SP

I know, I know, it's like suggesting your first love, right? But the Game Boy Advance SP, with its clamshell design and built-in backlight, is the epitome of retro handheld gaming. Isn't it comforting to know that in this high-tech world, we can still squint our eyes at a tiny screen and button mash our way through Pokemon Emerald for the umpteenth time?

2. Sega Game Gear

Who remembers the Sega Game Gear, and the six - yes, six - AA batteries it greedily devoured? It was ahead of its time with its color display and landscape design. Sonic never looked so good on the move. For the sake of your wallet and the environment, I'd recommend finding a rechargeable battery pack. The ozone layer doesn't really appreciate the Game Gear's insatiable battery appetite.

3. Neo Geo Pocket

The Neo Geo Pocket, the underdog that dared to challenge the Game Boy, and lost. But is it a loser in our hearts? Absolutely not. The 'clicky' stick and the fabulous selection of fighting games make this handheld a little gem for retro gaming fans. Who needs realistic MMA games when you have 16-bit SNK vs. Capcom?

4. Nintendo Switch Lite

Hear me out. The Nintendo Switch Lite may not strictly be 'retro' but with Nintendo's commitment to re-releasing classic titles, you can enjoy timeless games like Super Mario Bros., The Legend of Zelda, and Metroid in all their glory. Plus, it's got a battery life that can outlive most pet hamsters. Progress, right?

5. PSP Go

The PSP Go is sort of like the quirky cousin who shows up at the family reunion and hogs all the potato salad. This little handheld amalgamates retro gaming and digital media like no other. A slide-out control panel? Check. Digital game downloads? Check. The ability to play Final Fantasy VII on the move? Check and check.

6. Nintendo 3DS

The Nintendo 3DS allows players to experience 3D visuals without those annoying glasses. But we're here for the nostalgia, and the 3DS delivers that in spades with its Virtual Console. Is there anything more nostalgic than banking on nostalgia?

7. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx, renowned for being the first color handheld, makes its way onto our list. Let's be real: Playing California Games on a backlit, color screen was pretty groundbreaking stuff in the late 80s. It’s a fun addition to your collection, even if just for the bragging rights.

8. GP2X Wiz

Ever heard of the GP2X Wiz? Probably not, and that's what makes it so special. This open-source handheld was a dream come true for homebrew developers and emulation enthusiasts. It's like the Wild West of handhelds. "Ma, I'm off to play some homebrew games on my Wiz!" That's a sentence, I guarantee you, you won't hear very often.

9. Game Boy Micro

The Game Boy Micro, also known as the "Is that a gaming console or a matchbox?" This pint-sized handheld can easily slip into any pocket, except maybe the notoriously small ones in women's jeans. It’s a winner if you're a fan of very portable gaming, and don't mind looking like a giant while playing Super Mario Land.

10. PS Vita

And finally, the PS Vita. Poor, misunderstood Vita, victim of come-late-to-the-party syndrome. Despite its short-lived glory, the Vita has a great collection of retro and indie games. It's the swan song of Sony handhelds, a beautifully designed piece of tech that took Final Fantasy X on the road. It may not be retro, but it's retro-compatible, and that's a win in my book.

And there you have it, folks! Ten handheld consoles to satisfy all your mobile retro gaming needs. So, who needs the high-def cinematic gaming of 2023, when you can relive the glory days of gaming on these puppies? So whether you're a diehard Sega fan, a Nintendo nerd, or just want to play Tetris on an original Game Boy, there's a handheld for you. Game on!
