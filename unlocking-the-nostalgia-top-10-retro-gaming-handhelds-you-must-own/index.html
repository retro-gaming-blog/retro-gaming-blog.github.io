Unlocking the Nostalgia: Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Must Own

Ah, nostalgia. Isn't it just the best feeling? The sweet, earthy taste of the past, that takes you back to a time when life was simpler, when the only thing you had to worry about was how to beat the final boss on your favorite handheld game.

Whether you're a millennial with a soft spot for classic Nintendo or a Gen Z'er who appreciates the vintage aesthetic, retro gaming handhelds are a trip down memory lane that you just can't miss out on. And if you're asking yourself, "why on earth would I want to play these outdated pieces of tech when I have a PS5?", then my friend, this blog post is especially for you.

So, without further ado, buckle up for a journey back in time as we delve into the top 10 retro gaming handhelds you must own. And trust me, you're gonna want to grab a few of these bad boys!

1. Game Boy Advance SP

Remember when having a backlit screen was a luxury? Ah, those were the days. The Game Boy Advance SP was the cream of the crop, the big cheese, the...you get the point. With its clamshell design and rechargeable battery, it was an absolute game-changer. Plus, it played all your old Game Boy and Game Boy Color games. Talk about backward compatibility!

2. Nintendo DS Lite

And then came the DS Lite, the sleeker, sexier cousin of the DS. With two screens, touch capabilities, and a built-in microphone, it was like the James Bond of handhelds. The number of times I used that stylus to doodle in PictoChat—ah, those were the days.

3. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

Sony's answer to Nintendo's handheld dominance was the PSP. With its widescreen display and insane graphics (for the time), it was like having a portable PS2. I still have vivid memories of playing Grand Theft Auto: Vice City Stories during math class. Ah, to be young and reckless.

4. Game Boy Color

The Game Boy Color – the handheld that made us say "woah, games in color!". It was like Dorothy stepping into Oz. Suddenly, Pokémon wasn't just shades of gray. Pikachu was yellow, folks, YELLOW!

5. Sega Game Gear

The Game Gear was Sega's attempt to overthrow Nintendo's Game Boy. Although it didn't quite manage to dethrone the king, with its color screen and landscape design, it sure did give it a run for its money. And let's not forget those TV tuner add-ons. Watching TV on your Game Gear? Mind = blown.

6. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was that cool, alternative kid in school who listened to punk rock and had an edgy haircut. With its arcade-quality games and clicky joystick, it was way ahead of its time. Too bad it was so underrated.

7. Atari Lynx

The Lynx was like that one uncle who always had cool stuff but never really got the recognition he deserved. Being the first handheld with a color screen, it was ambitious but sadly overlooked. But hey, it was the first handheld console to feature network play. That’s something, right?

8. Game Boy Micro

The Game Boy Micro was the Game Boy Advance's little sibling that everyone forgot about. But let me tell you, this little guy was a trooper. With its tiny size and changeable faceplates, it was like the fashionista of handhelds.

9. Nintendo Game & Watch

Oh, the Game & Watch. These little gems were the precursors to the Game Boy, and boy, were they fun. Whether it was juggling balls or saving damsels in distress, these handhelds were simple but addictive. Plus, they doubled up as a clock. A game and a watch, get it?

10. PS Vita

Lastly, we have the PS Vita, Sony’s second shot at handheld gaming. And what a shot it was. With its touch capabilities, dual analog sticks, and stunning graphics, it was a powerhouse. Too bad Sony decided to abandon it. Rest in peace, sweet prince.

And there you have it, folks, the top 10 retro gaming handhelds you must own. So, dust off your old cartridges, pop in some batteries, and get ready to unlock the nostalgia. Trust me, you won’t regret it. And if anyone asks, just tell them you're being "retro".
