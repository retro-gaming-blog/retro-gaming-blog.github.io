Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Your Ultimate Guide to Vintage Gaming Fun

Oh, how time flies. One moment you're blowing into your Nintendo cartridge to make it work (which, by the way, never did anything beneficial), the next you're swiping through a touch screen, playing a game that looks like it's jumped straight out of a sci-fi movie. But let's be honest, nothing really beats the sheer nostalgia and simple joy of retro gaming, right? So, let's take a trip down memory lane with the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that will have you reminiscing about the good ol' days.

1. Game Boy

Can we even talk about retro gaming handhelds without mentioning the iconic Game Boy? I don't think so. This little gray brick was the ultimate symbol of coolness back in the day. With its green monochrome screen and that oh-so-catchy Tetris theme, the Game Boy was a trendsetter in portable gaming. And who can forget the intense battles in Pokemon Red and Blue? Fun fact: The Game Boy could run for up to 30 hours on just two AA batteries. Beat that, modern handhelds!

2. Game Boy Color

The Game Boy Color was like the younger, cooler sibling of the original Game Boy. It came with a color screen (hence the name, Sherlock) and a slightly sleeker design. Suddenly, our Pokemon battles got a lot more colorful!

3. Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance was the teenage rebel of the Game Boy family. With its landscape design and shoulder buttons, it was like nothing we'd seen before. Plus, it gave us some of the best games like 'The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap' and 'Metroid Fusion'. Ah, good times.

4. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was the smarty pants of handhelds. It had two screens, with the bottom one being touchscreen, and even a built-in microphone. It was like the future...in the past! And let's not forget the legendary 'Mario Kart DS' and 'New Super Mario Bros'. Those games were the stuff of legends.

5. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

The PSP was Sony's answer to Nintendo's handheld dominance. It was the cool kid on the block with its wide screen, analog stick, and ability to play movies and music. The PSP also boasted some amazing games like 'God of War: Chains of Olympus' and 'Grand Theft Auto: Liberty City Stories'. Now that's what I call versatility!

6. Game Gear

Remember Sega's attempt to dethrone Nintendo with the Game Gear? Yeah, me neither. All jokes aside, the Game Gear was a pretty solid handheld with its color screen and a decent library of games. Plus, you could watch TV on it with an additional tuner. Talk about ahead of its time!

7. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was the underdog of retro handhelds. It was the first handheld console with a color LCD and it even had backlighting (take that, Game Boy!). However, its high price and lack of games meant it never really took off. Still, it holds a special place in our retro gaming hearts.

8. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was like the indie darling of handhelds. It had some amazing games like 'SNK vs. Capcom: The Match of the Millennium' and 'Metal Slug: 1st Mission'. Plus, its clicky joystick was a joy to use. It's a shame it didn't get the success it deserved.

9. WonderSwan Color

The WonderSwan Color was Japan's secret weapon in the handheld wars. Created by the creator of the Game Boy (betrayal, much?), it was a pretty nifty handheld with a unique design and some great games. Unfortunately, it never made it to the west.

10. TurboExpress

Last but not least, we have the TurboExpress. This handheld was essentially a portable version of the TurboGrafx-16 console. It was expensive and had a terrible battery life, but it also had a color screen and could play all TurboGrafx-16 games. So, it was kind of a big deal.

And there you have it, folks! The top 10 retro gaming handhelds that defined a generation and gave us countless hours of fun. Sure, they might not have the graphics or the features of modern handhelds, but they had something far more important - character. So, dust off those old cartridges, grab some AA batteries, and let's get gaming!
