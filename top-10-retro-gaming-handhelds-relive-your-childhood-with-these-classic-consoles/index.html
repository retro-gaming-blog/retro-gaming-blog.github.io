Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Relive Your Childhood with these Classic Consoles

Ah, the good old days. Remember when you could play video games without being connected to the internet, without being bombarded by microtransactions, or without having to charge your device every two hours? I do. And let me tell you, those were the days! Let's take a nostalgic trip down memory lane, shall we? Here are the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that will make you feel like a kid again.

1. Game Boy

Let's start with the OG of handheld gaming, the Nintendo Game Boy. Released in 1989, this bad boy was as iconic as it gets. Who could forget the joy of popping in a fresh set of AA batteries and booting up a game of Tetris or Pokemon Red and Blue? Sure, the graphics were as basic as a pumpkin spice latte, but that was part of the charm, right?

2. Game Boy Color

Speaking of charm, how about the Game Boy Color? It was like the original Game Boy, but with... you guessed it, color! Suddenly, Pikachu wasn't just a bunch of black pixels on a green screen. He was yellow! Mind. Blown.

3. Game Boy Advance

Next up, the Game Boy Advance. This was the Game Boy's cooler, older brother. It had a wider screen, better graphics, and could play all the old Game Boy games. Plus, it had some killer titles of its own like Fire Emblem and Advance Wars. Remember when handheld games didn't need to have "mobile" in their title to be good? Yeah, me too.

4. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was the handheld that introduced us to the wonders of touch screen gaming. We all pretended to be cool with the stylus, but let's be honest, we all lost ours within the first week and just used our fingers instead.

5. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

The PSP was Sony's answer to the Game Boy's dominance. It was sleek, it was powerful, and it could play movies! Remember UMDs? Yeah, neither do I. But the games were top-notch, and that's what really mattered.

6. Sega Game Gear

Ah, the Sega Game Gear. This beast of a handheld was like the Hummer of the gaming world. It was big, it was bulky, and it ate batteries like nobody's business. But it had a color screen and could play Sonic on the go, so we all put up with it.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the hipster of handhelds. It wasn't as popular as the Game Boy or the Game Gear, but it had a cult following and some seriously good games. Plus, it had a clicky joystick, which was oddly satisfying.

8. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was the first handheld with color graphics. It was also the first handheld that you could play both left-handed and right-handed. Unfortunately, it was also the first handheld to be a commercial flop. But hey, it's not always about winning, right?

9. Nintendo Game & Watch

Before the Game Boy, there was the Game & Watch. These handhelds only played one game each, but they were the original portable gaming devices. Plus, they doubled as a clock, hence the name. Who needs a smartphone when you've got one of these?

10. TurboExpress

Last but not least, we have the TurboExpress. This handheld was basically a portable version of the TurboGrafx-16 console. It was expensive, it was rare, and it was totally rad. If you had one of these, you were definitely the coolest kid on the block.

So there you have it, folks. Ten retro gaming handhelds that will make you yearn for the simpler times. Times when games were games, handhelds were handhelds, and battery life was just a number. So dust off those old cartridges, pop in a fresh set of batteries, and get ready to relive your childhood. Because sometimes, the old ways are the best ways.
