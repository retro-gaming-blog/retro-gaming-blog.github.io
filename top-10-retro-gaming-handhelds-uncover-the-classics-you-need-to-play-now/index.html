Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Uncover the Classics You Need to Play Now

Well, hello there, fellow gamers! What's that? You thought handheld gaming started with the Nintendo Switch? Well, buckle up, because we're about to take a trip down memory lane to a time when pixels were king, and your game console could fit in your pocket (unless you were wearing skinny jeans, of course).

1. Game Boy

We can't start this list without the granddaddy of them all — the Nintendo Game Boy. Released in 1989, this chunky brick of a device was the gateway to portable gaming for many of us. Remember Tetris? Super Mario Land? The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening? Yeah, those were the days...

2. Game Boy Color

Nintendo wasn't content with just one Game Boy. Oh no, they had to go and make it in color! The Game Boy Color hit the scene in 1998 and was an instant success. Who could forget the first time they saw Pokémon in full color? I know I can't.

3. Game Boy Advance

Notice a pattern here? Yep, Nintendo was pretty much the king of handheld gaming back in the day. The Game Boy Advance, with its wide screen and shoulder buttons, was a game-changer (pun totally intended). Bonus points if you had the SP version with the flip screen and built-in rechargeable battery!

4. Nintendo DS

Just when you thought Nintendo was out of ideas, they hit us with the DS. Two screens? Touch controls? PictoChat? The DS was a mad scientist's dream come true, and we loved every crazy second of it.

5. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

Sony's foray into handheld gaming was a breath of fresh air. The PSP was sleek, stylish, and packed with power. It was like having a PlayStation 2 in your pocket. Plus, it played movies! UMDs, anyone?

6. Sega Game Gear

Let's not forget about Sega's contribution to the handheld gaming world. The Game Gear was their answer to the Game Boy, and it had a color screen before Nintendo even thought about it. Too bad it ate batteries like Pac-Man eats dots.

7. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was ahead of its time, with its color screen and ambidextrous design. Unfortunately, it was also expensive and didn't have a great selection of games. But hey, it's still a classic!

8. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the little handheld that could. It didn't have the backing of a major company like Nintendo or Sony, but it had some great games and a dedicated fanbase. Plus, it had a clicky joystick, which was pretty cool.

9. Nintendo 3DS

The 3DS was Nintendo's attempt to bring 3D gaming to the masses, without the need for those ridiculous glasses. It was a hit, with great games like Super Mario 3D Land and The Legend of Zelda: A Link Between Worlds. Plus, it had a built-in pedometer, so you could feel less guilty about all those hours spent gaming.

10. PS Vita

Last but not least, we have the PS Vita. Sony's second attempt at a handheld was a powerful device with a beautiful OLED screen. It didn't quite catch on like the PSP, but it's still a fantastic piece of hardware with a loyal following.

So there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds you need to play now (or, you know, whenever you can find them at a garage sale or on eBay). Sure, they might not have the fancy graphics or online capabilities of today's handhelds, but they've got something even better — nostalgia. And really, isn't that what gaming's all about?
