Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unleashing Nostalgia and Fun - Ultimate Buying Guide 2023

Hey there, gaming enthusiasts! Remember the days when a couple of AA batteries were the key to days of unadulterated gaming pleasure? Back in the days when screens were small, pixels were scarce, and yet the thrill of gaming was sky high? Now, that's the charm of retro gaming handhelds! In this post, we're going to take a trip down memory lane and explore some of the best retro handhelds that you can (and should) lay your hands on in 2023.

1. Game Boy Advance SP

It's a bird... It's a plane... No, it's the Game Boy Advance SP! Who doesn't remember this hinged wonder? With its rechargeable battery (big deal back then, trust me), this baby was a game-changer.

2. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Sony's PSP was basically a PlayStation 2 for your pocket. Grand Theft Auto on the bus, anyone? No? Just me? Alright...

3. Sega Game Gear

Who else burned through six AA batteries for 3 hours of Sonic the Hedgehog? No regrets though. The Game Gear was the color-TV to Game Boy's radio, despite its insatiable hunger for batteries.

4. Nintendo DS Lite

With its dual screens and touch input, the DS Lite was a breath of fresh air. And it came in fancy colors too. Candy Apple Red, anyone?

5. Atari Lynx

The Lynx was a beast. It was the first handheld with color and backlighting. Plus, it was ambidextrous! Left-handed gamers, this one’s for you!

6. Neo Geo Pocket Color

SNK's Neo Geo Pocket Color was the underdog that could. It brought arcade-like experiences to the handheld market. And yes, it had a clicky joystick. Sweet!

7. Nintendo 3DS

Does the Nintendo 3DS really need an introduction? It's like the DS, but with 3D. Does it give you a headache? Maybe. Is it worth it? Definitely!

8. Sega Nomad

Sega's Nomad was essentially a portable Genesis. You could play your Genesis games on the go. Why didn't it catch on? Well, it ate batteries like Pac-Man eats dots.

9. WonderSwan Color

Bandai's WonderSwan Color gave Nintendo a run for their money in Japan. It had great games and a, well, wonderful design. Too bad it never made it to the West.

10. PS Vita

Last but not least, the PS Vita. A beautiful OLED screen, dual analog sticks, and a ton of great games. If only Sony hadn't forgotten about it...

So there you have it, folks, the top 10 retro gaming handhelds to check out in 2023. Whether you're looking to relive the glory days of 8-bit gaming or finally beating that level of Crash Bandicoot on the go, these handhelds have got you covered.

Whether you're a hardcore gamer looking to revisit the classics or a casual player looking for a dose of nostalgia, these retro gaming handhelds offer something for everyone. So why wait? Go get your hands on one of these and let the nostalgia trip begin!

Remember, in the world of gaming, it’s not about the newest, flashiest tech. Sometimes, it’s about the feeling of satisfaction you get from finally smashing that one difficult level on your Game Boy after weeks of trying. It’s about the simple joy of seeing your character evolve in a Pokémon game.

Retro gaming handhelds bring back that pure joy and simplicity, making them an essential possession for any and every gaming enthusiast - which, if you've made it this far into the post, is definitely you. Happy gaming, folks!
