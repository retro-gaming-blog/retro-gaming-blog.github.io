Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Should Own: A Comprehensive Guide

Hey there, fellow joystick jockeys! Time to put down your slick, shiny, new-age handhelds for just a moment and take a step back - way back - into the glorious era of retro gaming handhelds. For those not in the know, we're talking about those bulky, pixelated, monochrome wonders that came before the advent of high-definition touch screens and augmented reality. You know, the ones that kept us entertained on those long family roadtrips when the only 'mobile gaming' we knew was trying not to drop our devices while the car was moving.

Game Boy

Let's start with the majestic beast that wasn't just a gaming handheld, but a whole mood. Nintendo's Game Boy was the Mack Daddy of portable gaming. I mean, who can forget the sheer joy of playing 'Super Mario Land' on that pea-soup green, pixelated screen? I could almost smell the mushrooms and turtle shells. It was big, it was heavy, and it ate batteries for breakfast. But, hey, that was half the fun, right?

Game Gear

Ever thought to yourself, "Hey, I really wish I could take my Sega Genesis with me wherever I go?" No? Just me? Well, joke's on you because Sega Game Gear was exactly that: a portable Sega Genesis. It might not have had the battery life of a toddler's attention span, but it sure was a treat for the eyes with its full-color capabilities.

Atari Lynx

What list of retro gaming handhelds would be complete without the Atari Lynx? If the Game Boy was the popular jock of handheld consoles, then the Lynx was the nerdy genius on the chess team. It was the first gaming handheld with a color LCD, but as we all know, being ahead of your time doesn't always mean you'll be the most popular. But we still love you, Lynx, in all your misunderstood glory.

Neo Geo Pocket

Neo Geo Pocket? More like Neo Geo Rocket! This pocket-sized powerhouse had a stick instead of a D-pad, which was a breath of fresh air after all those blisters from the Game Boy buttons. But let's be real, the true charm of the Neo Geo Pocket was trying to convince your friends that your obscure Japanese handheld was cooler than their mainstream Game Boy.

Game Boy Color

When Nintendo finally decided to color our world with the Game Boy Color, it was like Christmas morning. All your favorite classics, now in color! Remember the first time you saw Pokemon Red and Blue in actual red and blue? Mind-blowing.

WonderSwan

Judging by the name, you'd think the WonderSwan was some kind of elegant, swan-like creature from Willy Wonka’s factory. In reality, it was a playfully designed handheld where you could switch between horizontal and vertical game styles. Only in the 90s, folks.

Game Boy Advance

How could the Game Boy possibly get any better? Well, by advancing, of course. The Game Boy Advance was like the older brother who went to college, got a degree, and came back home all sophisticated and worldly. The horizontal design was a game changer, and the library of games was nothing short of spectacular.

Nintendo DS

Two screens, touch controls, a microphone - the Nintendo DS was Nintendo saying, "Hold my beer" to all its previous shortcomings. It's not that retro, but it's certainly a remarkable evolution in the world of handheld gaming.

PSP

Sony’s PlayStation Portable (PSP) was like the handheld straight out of a sci-fi movie. Smooth design, widescreen display, and a plethora of games - the PSP was Sony showing off, and we absolutely loved it.

Sega Nomad

Finally, the Sega Nomad. It's on our list for the sole reason of being the most absurdly amazing handheld ever created. Why has it earned this title, you ask? Well, the Nomad allowed you to play literal Sega Genesis cartridges. It's like if Nintendo made a handheld console where you can shove your full-sized NES games. Absolutely bonkers and fantastic.

And there you have it - the top 10 retro gaming handhelds you should own. Not for the superior graphics or the touch controls or the Wi-Fi capabilities, but for the sheer old-school charm they bring along. After all, nostalgia is and always will be the best game to play. Until next time, game on, folks!
