Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Nostalgic Powerhouse in 2023

"In the world of gaming, it's not about the graphics. It's about the gameplay." - Shigeru Miyamoto (probably)

Alright, buckle up my pixel-loving friends because today we're going on a journey. A journey back in time. A journey that's not about teraflops, gigabytes, or the latest ray-tracing technology. No, this journey is all about that sweet, sweet nostalgia. We're talking about the top 10 retro gaming handhelds.

1. Game Boy (1989)

Ah, the Game Boy. Who can forget the gray brick with its spinach-green screen and those sweet, sweet chiptunes? You could drop this baby from a 10-story building and it would still play Tetris like a champ. It's the OG and still a solid choice for retro gaming in 2023.

2. Game Gear (1990)

Did you like the Game Boy but thought, "Nah, I need something bigger and with a color screen?" Well, Sega heard your oddly specific request and gave us the Game Gear. Sure, it eats batteries faster than Pac-Man eats pellets, but with games like Sonic the Hedgehog and Streets of Rage, it's totally worth it.

3. Game Boy Color (1998)

Alright, so maybe you wanted that Game Boy charm but with a little more color. Enter the Game Boy Color. It's like the Game Boy had a baby with a rainbow. And it brought us Pokemon Gold and Silver so, yeah, it's basically amazing.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color (1999)

Who was this for? No, seriously, who actually had one of these? Well, if you did, you were one of the lucky few who got to experience this hidden gem. It had some killer fighting games, and unlike the Game Gear, the battery life was actually decent.

5. Game Boy Advance (2001)

Okay, so we've talked about the Game Boy, but have you met its cooler, sleeker younger brother? The Game Boy Advance was like the Ferrari of handheld consoles back in the day. With a 32-bit processor, it could play games that looked almost as good as the SNES. Plus, you could play all your old Game Boy games on it. What's not to love?

6. Nintendo DS (2004)

You know what's better than one screen? Two screens! Enter the Nintendo DS. This dual-screened wonder brought us touch-based gaming before the iPhone made it cool. Plus, it had a built-in microphone for those intense Nintendogs sessions.

7. PSP (2004)

"Hey, you got your PlayStation in my pocket!" That's right, Sony decided to shrink down the PS2 and put it in your hands with the PSP. It was pretty powerful for a handheld, and it had a lot of great games. Too bad about that UMD format, though.

8. Game & Watch (1980)

This one is for the true retro lovers out there. Before the Game Boy, before the NES, there was the Game & Watch. These little handhelds each had just one game, but they were the start of Nintendo's foray into handheld gaming. Plus, they introduced us to a little character named Mario.

9. Atari Lynx (1989)

Ah, the Atari Lynx, the handheld that could have been. It was the first handheld with a color screen, and it had some pretty good games. Unfortunately, it was also huge and expensive. But hey, it's still a cool piece of gaming history.

10. Wonderswan Color (2000)

Finally, we have the Wonderswan Color. This Japan-only handheld was pretty unique. It had both portrait and landscape modes, and it had some really cool games. If you can get your hands on one, it's definitely worth checking out.

And there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds in 2023. Sure, we've got 4K, VR, and all sorts of fancy tech nowadays. But sometimes, it's good to just take a step back and appreciate where we came from. So, grab your Game Boy, fire up some Super Mario Land, and let the nostalgia wash over you. After all, it's not about the graphics. It's about the gameplay.
