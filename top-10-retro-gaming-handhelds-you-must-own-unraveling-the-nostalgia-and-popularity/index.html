Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Must Own: Unraveling the Nostalgia and Popularity

Ah, the good old days. Remember when you could play a game without an internet connection? Or when you didn't have to sell your kidney to afford the latest console? Yes, we're talking about the golden age of handheld gaming. Let's take a trip down memory lane and explore the top 10 retro gaming handhelds you absolutely must own.

1. Game Boy

We're kicking things off with a classic - the Nintendo Game Boy. If you didn't own one of these bad boys, were you even a '90s kid? This chunky little device was the epitome of cool, despite its monochrome screen. And who can forget the thrill of swapping those game cartridges? Tetris, anyone?

2. Game Boy Color

Just when we thought the Game Boy couldn't get any cooler, Nintendo hit us with the Game Boy Color. Suddenly, our favorite games were bursting with color. It was like Dorothy stepping into Oz for the first time. Pure magic!

3. Game Boy Advance

And then came the Game Boy Advance. It was sleek, it was modern, and it was horizontal. This was the handheld that introduced us to the joys of shoulder buttons. And let's not forget the sheer joy of playing Pok√©mon Ruby and Sapphire on this device.

4. Nintendo DS

Two screens? Check. Touchscreen capabilities? Check. The ability to play old Game Boy Advance games? Double check. The Nintendo DS was a game-changer, literally. It was like having a mini laptop, but for games. Genius!

5. Sony PSP

Who said handheld gaming was just for kids? The Sony PSP was the sophisticated older sibling in the handheld family. With its sleek design and impressive graphics, it was a must-have for any self-respecting gamer. Plus, it played movies. On a handheld!

6. Sega Game Gear

Remember the Sega Game Gear? No? That's because you were probably too busy playing your Game Boy. But this underrated gem was way ahead of its time. It had a color screen years before the Game Boy Color. Plus, it had a TV tuner. A TV tuner!

7. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was another handheld that was ahead of its time. It was the first handheld with a color LCD and the ability to connect up to 17 units for multiplayer games. Unfortunately, it was also the size of a brick. But hey, no pain, no gain, right?

8. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the handheld for the discerning gamer. Sure, it didn't have the same range of games as the Game Boy. But the games it did have were top-notch. Plus, it had a clicky joystick. A clicky joystick!

9. Nintendo Game & Watch

Before the Game Boy, there was the Game & Watch. These handhelds were the original portable gaming devices. Each one featured a single game, like Donkey Kong or Mario's Cement Factory. They were simple, but oh so addictive.

10. Turbo Express

Last, but not least, we have the Turbo Express. This handheld was basically a portable version of the TurboGrafx-16 console. It even played the same game cards. It was like carrying a console in your pocket. Mind blown!

So there you have it, folks. A trip down memory lane with the top 10 retro gaming handhelds you must own. Whether you're a seasoned gamer or a nostalgic collector, these devices are sure to bring a smile to your face. And who knows? Maybe they'll even inspire you to dust off your old Game Boy and play a round of Tetris. For old times' sake.
