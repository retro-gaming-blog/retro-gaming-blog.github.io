Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Classics You Need to Own Now

Listen up, fellow gaming connoisseurs. We're about to travel back in time, grab some of the best handheld gaming classics, and bring them back to 2023. I mean, who needs all these high-definition, virtual reality gizmos when you could be navigating the streets of Super Mario Land, right?

1. Game Boy

Let's start with the granddaddy of them all, the Game Boy. Now, I'm not talking about the fancy Game Boy Advance or the sleek Game Boy SP, I mean the original brick-like Game Boy from 1989. Monochrome graphics, bulky buttons, and a battery life that could rival the Energizer bunny. And Tetris, let's not forget the glorious hours spent rotating blocks and eradicating rows.

2. Game Gear

Second on our list is Game Gear. Sega's answer to Nintendo's Game Boy. It had a brightly colored backlit screen that outshone its competition, but its battery life was as limited as my motivation for housework. The battles in Sonic Triple Trouble alone are worth the price of admission and frequent battery purchases.

3. Atari Lynx

Next up, Atari Lynx. This was the first handheld with color graphics, my friends. It was like stepping out of Kansas and into Oz – a big deal at the time. Plus, it was ambidextrous before it was cool, catering to both left and right-handed gamers. Too bad they didn't think of creating a battery saver mode though.

4. TurboExpress

Don’t let the name fool you. TurboExpress was like the sports car of handhelds. The Rolls Royce, if you will. It could play all TurboGrafx-16 games, something no other handheld could boast of at the time. However, the price tag was sky-high, so unless you saved up your birthday money for, let's say, a decade, this baby was off-limits.

5. Neo Geo Pocket

The Neo Geo Pocket. This maverick had not one, but two versions. What started as a monochrome system quickly leveled up to a color platform. Geared towards fighting games, it was the perfect handheld for those looking to channel their inner Ryu or Ken. Hadouken!

6. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS often gets overlooked as a "retro" console, but remember that this bad boy came out in 2004. It’s crazy, right? It featured a dual-screen, a built-in microphone, and touch-screen controls. It was like the Swiss Army knife of gaming handhelds.

7. Sega Nomad

Remember the Sega Nomad? This was the original Nintendo Switch letting us play full-sized Genesis games. However, its battery life was so short, it could be used as a metaphor for the fleeting nature of happiness.

8. Game Boy Color

Enter the Game Boy Color, the younger, hipper sibling of the original Game Boy. It took all the charm of the original and added a splash of color. Plus, Pokémon Yellow, Blue, and Red looked so much better in color, wouldn't you agree?

9. Sony PSP

Ah, the Sony PSP, a beast in handheld gaming. It was like holding a portable PS2. It had Wi-Fi capabilities, a media player, and even internet browsers. It was so advanced that I'm fairly certain it could make a cup of coffee if you asked it nicely.

10. Game and Watch

Finally, we have Game and Watch. These were single-game devices but they paved the way for what was to come. Plus, they doubled as digital watches. In the 80's, this was as high-tech as Marty McFly's hoverboard.

So there you have it, folks. These are the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that you absolutely need to own, like, yesterday. If you don't already have these in your collection, what are you waiting for? Get out there and start scouring the internet or your local game store. It's time to re-live the glory days, one pixel at a time. Happy gaming!
