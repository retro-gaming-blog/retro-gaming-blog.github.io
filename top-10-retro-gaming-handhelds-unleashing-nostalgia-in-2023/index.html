Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unleashing Nostalgia in 2023!

Ah, 2023. The year of hoverboards, self-lacing shoes, and...retro gaming handhelds? Yep, you heard it right. In the era of the PlayStation 5, the Xbox Series X, and whatever Nintendo's cooking up next, we've decided to take a trip down memory lane. Because why not?

1. Game Boy Color

Let's start with the granddaddy of them all, the Game Boy Color. It's like the Beatles of handheld gaming - everyone has heard of it, and it's probably influenced your favorite handheld in some way. And come on, who can resist the charm of that pea soup green screen and chunky plastic casing?

2. Nintendo DS

Next up is the Nintendo DS, the Game Boy's cooler, younger sibling. With its dual screens and touch capabilities, the DS was a game-changer (pun absolutely intended). Remember when we all used to blow into our cartridges to make them work? Ah, those were the days.

3. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Ah, the PlayStation Portable, aka the PSP. Sony's first foray into handheld gaming was a solid hit, boasting impressive graphics and a sleek design. Who didn't feel like a total boss playing 'Grand Theft Auto' on the bus ride home?

4. Sega Game Gear

The Sega Game Gear is like the forgotten middle child of retro handhelds. Overshadowed by its Nintendo counterparts, the Game Gear was nonetheless a gem in its own right. Plus, it had a backlit screen – a major luxury back in the day!

5. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx, the first handheld with color graphics. Yeah, you heard that right. Color. Graphics. In a handheld. In the 1980s. Mind blown, right?

6. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was like the indie darling of handhelds. Despite being relatively unknown, it had a dedicated following and a robust library of games. Plus, it was the first to introduce a clicky thumbstick – a feature we now take for granted.

7. Game Boy Advance SP

The Game Boy Advance SP was like the Game Boy Color on steroids. With a clamshell design and a backlit screen, it was like the Cadillac of handhelds. And don't even get me started on the nostalgia of playing 'Pokemon Emerald' on this bad boy.

8. Nintendo 3DS

The Nintendo 3DS, where 3D actually worked...sort of. Despite giving some of us headaches, the 3D effect was pretty cool. Plus, it's backward compatible with DS games, making it a must-have for any retro gaming enthusiast.

9. PlayStation Vita

The PlayStation Vita, the PSP's younger, more sophisticated sibling. With its dual analog sticks and touch capabilities, the Vita was a powerhouse. Plus, it had some killer exclusives like 'Persona 4 Golden' and 'Uncharted: Golden Abyss'.

10. Nintendo Switch Lite

Last but certainly not least, the Nintendo Switch Lite. Okay, so maybe it's not exactly "retro" yet, but give it a few years. With its versatile library of games and portable design, it's sure to be a classic in no time.

So there you have it, folks. Ten retro gaming handhelds that can transport you back to simpler times. Times when all you needed was a handful of AA batteries and a good game to keep you entertained for hours. So dust off those old cartridges, fire up those screens, and get ready to unleash some serious nostalgia in 2023! Because who needs hoverboards when you've got a Game Boy?
