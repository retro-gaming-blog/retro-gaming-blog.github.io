Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Classics that Shaped our Gaming History

Who needs the latest PS5 or Xbox Series X when you've got a pocket full of nostalgia? That's right folks, today we're diving headfirst into the pixelated pool of retro gaming handhelds. So, grab your double A batteries and let's embark on a journey back in time. You ready? Let's go!

1. Game Boy

Ah, the Game Boy! Who could forget the chunky grey brick that introduced us to the world of portable gaming? With its monochrome screen and killer titles like 'Super Mario Land', 'Tetris', and 'The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening', the Game Boy was the handheld that started it all. I mean, who needs color when you've got 8-bit charm, right?

2. Game Boy Color

Speaking of color, Nintendo didn't stop at the Game Boy. No sir, they gave us the Game Boy Color. Remember the sheer joy of seeing your favorite characters in color for the first time? I sure do. Pokemon Yellow in full color? Yes, please!

3. Game Boy Advance

But wait, there's more! Remember the Game Boy Advance? This bad boy was all the rage back in the early 2000s. With its horizontal design and extensive library (hello, 'Advance Wars' and 'Fire Emblem'), the GBA was a game changer.

4. Sega Game Gear

Now, let's not forget about Sega. The Game Gear was Sega's answer to the Game Boy, and boy did it deliver. Yeah sure, it was a bit bulkier and had a criminally short battery life, but who cares when you've got a backlit color screen and 'Sonic the Hedgehog' on the go?

5. Nintendo DS

Ah, the Nintendo DS, the handheld that had us all touching our games for the first time. Remember how mind-blowing it was to play 'Brain Age' and 'Nintendogs' using a stylus? And don't get me started on the 'Mario Kart DS' multiplayer battles. Good times!

6. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

Sony's PSP was a beast of a handheld. It had a huge screen, impressive graphics, and some stellar games ('God of War: Chains of Olympus', anyone?). And who could forget the UMDs? Those little discs were a pain to handle, but they sure looked cool.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was a bit of an underdog in the handheld scene, but it was a gem nonetheless. With a solid lineup of fighting games and a clicky joystick, the NGPC was a dream come true for arcade lovers. It may not have been as popular as the Game Boy, but it sure had a lot of heart.

8. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was a handheld ahead of its time. With its ambidextrous design and color screen, the Lynx was truly innovative. It may not have had the biggest library, but games like 'Blue Lightning' and 'Chip's Challenge' left a lasting impression.

9. Nintendo Game & Watch

The granddaddy of them all, the Nintendo Game & Watch was where it all started. These little handhelds didn't just tell the time, they introduced us to the joys of digital gaming. Playing 'Ball' or 'Donkey Kong' on these was a rite of passage for any gamer worth their salt.

10. TurboExpress

And finally, we have the TurboExpress. This handheld was basically a portable version of the TurboGrafx-16 console. It was expensive and ate batteries for breakfast, but it offered console-quality gaming on the go. If that's not impressive, I don't know what is.

So there you have it, folks. Ten handhelds that defined our gaming history. Whether you were a Nintendo kid, a Sega fan, or a Sony devotee, there's no denying the impact these handhelds had on our lives. So, here's to the joy of gaming on the go. Long live the handhelds!
