Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Best Kept Secrets of the 80s and 90s

You know what they say, old is gold, right? Ah, the 80s and 90s! The golden era of gaming handhelds. Back then, we didn't have fancy smartphones with high-end graphics. No, we had these chunky, noisy, pixelated little devices that brought us immense joy and an occasional bout of frustration. So, let's take a nostalgic trip down the memory lane and look at the top 10 retro gaming handhelds from the 80s and 90s. Buckle up, there's going to be a lot of "I remember that!" moments.

1. Game Boy

No list of retro handhelds would be complete without the one, the only, the legend itself - the Game Boy. Released in 1989 by Nintendo, Game Boy was a revolution in handheld gaming. I mean, who can forget those hours spent playing Tetris or Super Mario Land? Fun fact, did you know that Game Boy's designer was also the designer of the Metroid series? Talk about a double whammy!

2. Sega Game Gear

Not to be outdone, Sega gave its own attempt at handheld gaming with the Game Gear. Remember that sleek black design and color screen? Yeah, it ate batteries like a hungry bear, but that was a small price to pay for the pleasure of playing Sonic on-the-go.

3. Atari Lynx

While we are on the topic of color screens, let's bring up the Atari Lynx. It was the first handheld console with a color LCD. Innovative? Absolutely. Successful? Well, not so much. But it's worth a mention for its daring attempt.

4. TurboExpress

TurboExpress was like the Rolls Royce of gaming handhelds, luxurious and oh-so-expensive. A portable console that could play home console games? Unheard of! It was the closest many got to owning a portable TV in the 90s.

5. Neo Geo Pocket

Who here remembers the SNK's Neo Geo Pocket? Released in the late 90s, this little gaming device was Sega and Nintendo's competitor. With a joystick instead of a D-pad and a decent library of games, Neo Geo Pocket had its fan base.

6. Game Boy Color

If Game Boy was a revolution, then Game Boy Color was a revelation. It brought the joy of color to our favorite Game Boy titles. Pokemon Gold and Silver, anyone?

7. WonderSwan

WonderSwan, despite its strange name, was a formidable contender. Made by the creator of Game Boy, this handheld was only released in Japan. Tragic, right?

8. Nintendo GameBoy Advance

The GameBoy Advance or the GBA was the sleek, angular successor to the Game Boy Color. It brought a wide library of games and superior graphics. Trust me, nothing felt as good as playing Mario Kart on this thing.

9. Sega Nomad

Sega's Nomad was a portable Genesis console. Yes, you heard it right! Though it was more or less a commercial failure, for those lucky enough to own one, it was a pocket of joy.

10. Sony PocketStation

Last but not least, Sony's PocketStation. This was more of a memory card than a handheld gaming device. Its miniature screen and single action button were certainly limiting, but it was a bold experiment.

There you have it, a stroll down the memory lane with the top 10 retro gaming handhelds. Now, wasn't that a blast from the past? It's amazing to see how far we've come from these pixelated, monochromatic screens to today's high-definition gaming devices. But, let's be real, nothing can match the charm and nostalgia of these retro gaming handhelds. No matter how many advancements come our way, we gamers will always have a soft spot for the good old days of the 80s and 90s. So, here's to the relentless spirit of gaming and all the memories these retro handhelds gave us! Cheers!
