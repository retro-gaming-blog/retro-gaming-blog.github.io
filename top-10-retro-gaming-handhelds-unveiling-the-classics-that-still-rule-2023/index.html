Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Classics That Still Rule 2023

Let's take a moment to appreciate the good old days, shall we? You know what I'm talking about. Those were the days when the graphics were simple, the sound was beepy, and the gameplay was so addictive that you'd willingly sacrifice sleep and chores just to complete that one level. Yeah, I'm talking about retro gaming handhelds, my friends. They may not have the flashy graphics or the cinematic narratives of today's games, but they sure do have a charm that keeps us coming back. So, let's dive into the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that still rule in 2023. Buckle up, it's going to be a nostalgic ride!

1. The Game Boy

First on the list, and rightly so, is the one and only Game Boy. This iconic piece of hardware from Nintendo was a revolution in the gaming industry. With its chunky grey body and greenish screen, it wasn't much to look at, but boy did it pack a punch. Who can forget the thrill of playing Tetris or Pok√©mon Red and Blue for the first time? The Game Boy is the granddaddy of handhelds, and it still holds a special place in our hearts.

2. Game Gear

Next up is Sega's answer to the Game Boy - the Game Gear. This sleek black machine was the James Bond of handhelds. It came with a color screen and a backlit display, features that were ahead of its time. Sure, it ate batteries like a hungry hippo, but for the chance to play Sonic on the go? Totally worth it.

3. Atari Lynx

Atari Lynx, oh how you tried. This handheld was a beast, with its color screen and the ability to link up with other Lynxes for multiplayer action. It was also the first handheld to introduce us to the concept of left-handed gaming. A feature appreciated by our left-handed brethren. But, alas, it couldn't compete with the Game Boy's popularity. Still, it has a cult following to this day.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color, or NGPC as the cool kids call it, was a gem of a handheld. With its joystick and two-button layout, it was a dream for fighting game enthusiasts. Games like Samurai Shodown and King of Fighters felt right at home on the NGPC. It may not have been a commercial success, but it won the hearts of many gamers.

5. Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance, or GBA for short, was the Game Boy's cooler, more sophisticated younger brother. With its wider screen and shoulder buttons, it brought console-quality games to our pockets. Remember playing Advance Wars or Metroid Fusion? Those were the days.

6. PlayStation Portable

The PlayStation Portable, or PSP, was Sony's first foray into the handheld market, and what a debut it was. It was like having a PlayStation 2 in your hands, complete with the ability to watch movies and listen to music. It was more than a gaming device; it was a multimedia powerhouse.

7. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was a game-changer, literally. With its two screens, touch controls, and a built-in microphone, it opened up new possibilities for gameplay. Games like Brain Age and Nintendogs were unlike anything we'd seen before. The DS proved that innovation and fun could go hand in hand.

8. Sega Nomad

The Sega Nomad was essentially a portable Sega Genesis. Yes, you read that right. You could play your favorite Genesis games on the go. It was a bit hefty and battery life was not its strong suit, but it was a portable Genesis. 'Nuff said.

9. Game Boy Color

The Game Boy Color, or GBC, was the Game Boy's colorful cousin. It brought our favorite games to life with vibrant colors. Plus, it was backward compatible with all the original Game Boy games. It was like getting a whole new gaming library for free!

10. TurboExpress

Rounding out our list is the TurboExpress, a handheld that was essentially a portable TurboGrafx-16. It had a high price tag and a short battery life, but it offered an unmatched gaming experience. Games like Bonk's Adventure and Alien Crush looked amazing on its sharp screen.

So, there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds that still rule in 2023. They may be relics of a bygone era, but they continue to captivate us with their charm and nostalgia. So, dust off those old cartridges, pop in some batteries, and let's get gaming. After all, who needs sleep when you have Tetris to conquer?
