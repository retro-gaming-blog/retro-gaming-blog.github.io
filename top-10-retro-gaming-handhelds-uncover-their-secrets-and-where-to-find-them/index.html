Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Uncover their Secrets and Where to Find Them

Oh, the nostalgia of retro gaming handhelds. Remember when the pixelated plumber jumped on a turtle and we all thought it was the best thing since sliced bread? Or that time when you were desperately trying to catch a rare Pikachu on your Game Boy Color, under the blanket, in the middle of the night? Ah, the good old times.

Today, we're going on a trip down memory lane, exploring 10 of the best retro gaming handhelds ever made. We're not just talking about the consoles themselves, but the secrets they hold and where you can find them. Buckle up, this is going to be a ride!

1. Game Boy

Ah, the Game Boy, the handheld that started it all. Who can forget the thrill of booting it up for the first time? Or the horror of dropping it only to find out it was built like a tank and could survive anything? This little grey box of joy held secrets like "The Konami Code" in games like Contra, or the MissingNo glitch in Pokemon Red and Blue. Want one now? Garage sales and local flea markets are your best bet.

2. Game Boy Color

If Game Boy was the revolution, Game Boy Color was the evolution. Same old, same old – but now in color! And who can forget the mystery of the Cinnabar Island glitch in Pokemon games? eBay or local second-hand stores are your go-to for this colorful piece of gaming history.

3. Game Boy Advance

Game Boy Advance, a.k.a GBA, is like Game Boy's cooler, more stylish sibling. It had a wide variety of games, but the best secret? The hidden NES games in Animal Crossing. Thrift stores and online marketplaces are where you can hunt down this piece of nostalgia.

4. Sega Game Gear

Remember Sega Game Gear? Of course you do! It was the Technicolor dream to Game Boy's monochrome reality. How about that secret level in Sonic Triple Trouble? Try finding one of these babies at a vintage toy store or online.

5. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Neo Geo Pocket Color, the underdog of handheld consoles, actually had some killer games. For instance, SNK vs Capcom: The Match of the Millennium, was a secret fighting game fan's paradise. For this gem, check out pawn shops or online retro gaming stores.

6. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was like a breath of fresh air in the gaming world. Remember finding the secret star in Super Mario 64 DS? Retro gaming exhibitions and online auctions are the places to look for a Nintendo DS.

7. PSP (PlayStation Portable)

Sony's PSP was the cool kid on the block with its sleek design and robust game library. Remember the hidden Easter Eggs in Liberty City Stories? Your best bet to score a PSP these days is at a used electronics store or online marketplaces.

8. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx, an unsung hero of the handheld world, was actually the first with a color LCD. Remember the hidden credits screen in Scrapyard Dog? To get hold of one, try visiting a retro gaming fair or searching online.

9. TurboExpress

TurboExpress, the handheld version of the TurboGrafx-16 console, was a beast. It even played the same game cards! Remember the secret room in Bonk's Adventure? To get a TurboExpress, your best bet is to hit up a secondhand electronics store or scour online classifieds.

10. WonderSwan

Finally, the WonderSwan. Released only in Japan, this handheld had a unique design and was even compatible with an add-on that allowed you to tune into FM radio stations! Finding one outside of Japan might be a bit tricky, but not impossible – try looking at international online marketplaces or at a Japanese retro game shop.

And there you have it, folks. Ten retro gaming handhelds that made our childhoods magical, and still hold a special place in our hearts. Sure, they might not have the flashy graphics and complex mechanics of modern games, but they've got something far more valuable – memories. So why not dust off that old Game Boy and relive the magic? After all, they don't make 'em like they used to, right?
