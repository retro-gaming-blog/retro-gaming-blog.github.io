Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Uncover the Classics and Where to Find Them

Ah, retro gaming handhelds. They're like a time machine, taking us back to the simpler days of our childhood. And let's be honest, who doesn't love a good dose of nostalgia? So put on your nostalgia goggles, folks, because we're about to take a trip down memory lane.

1. Game Boy

Let's start with the granddaddy of them all: the Game Boy. Released by Nintendo in 1989, this little brick of a device was an absolute game-changer (pun intended). Remember the sheer joy of playing Tetris or Pokémon for the first time? Or the frustration when the batteries died in the middle of a game? Ah, those were the days.

2. Game Boy Color

Not to be outdone by its older sibling, the Game Boy Color arrived in 1998 with a bang. With its ability to display a whopping 56 colors at once, it was like we'd stepped into the future. And who could forget the sheer panic of losing that tiny cartridge of Pokémon Gold or Silver?

3. Game Boy Advance

Continuing the Game Boy legacy, the Game Boy Advance (2001) was a sleek, horizontal design that felt like holding a mini spaceship. With games like Metroid Fusion and The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap, it was a must-have for any self-respecting gamer.

4. Nintendo DS

Is it a bird? Is it a plane? No, it's the Nintendo DS! With its dual screens and stylus, the DS (2004) was a revolution in handheld gaming. Who knew that poking a screen could be so much fun?

5. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

In 2005, Sony decided to crash Nintendo's party with the PlayStation Portable, or PSP for short. With its large screen and impressive graphics, it was like having a PS2 in your pocket. Plus, it played movies. Movies, people!

6. Atari Lynx

Let's not forget about the Atari Lynx (1989). With its color screen and ability to be played left-handed, it was a trendsetter in its own right. Too bad it was as big as a house brick and ate batteries like a hungry, hungry hippo.

7. Sega Game Gear

The Sega Game Gear (1990) was Sega's answer to the Game Boy. With a color screen and a library of games like Sonic the Hedgehog, it was a worthy contender. Too bad it also had a voracious appetite for batteries.

8. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color (1999) was a bit of an underdog in the handheld gaming world. With its clicky joystick and solid game library, it was a hidden gem. If you ever come across one, don't hesitate to snatch it up.

9. WonderSwan Color

The WonderSwan Color (2000) was another underrated handheld. Released only in Japan, it had a unique vertical/horizontal design and an impressive library of games. If you're ever in Japan, be sure to look for it in second-hand stores.

10. Nintendo 3DS

Last but not least, we have the Nintendo 3DS (2011). With its 3D screen and backward compatibility with DS games, it was a hit with gamers. Plus, it has a sleep mode so you don't lose your progress when the battery dies. What a concept!

So there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds. If you're a fan of nostalgia and gaming, be sure to keep an eye out for these classics. You never know when you might stumble upon a piece of gaming history.

But remember, while these handhelds may not have the graphics or processing power of today's gaming devices, they have something far more important: heart. And isn't that what gaming is really all about?
