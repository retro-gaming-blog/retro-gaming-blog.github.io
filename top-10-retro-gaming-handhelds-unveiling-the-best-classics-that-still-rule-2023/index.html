Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Best Classics That Still Rule 2023

Hello, gamers! I trust we're all well and locked in our gaming dens, because today we're diving headfirst into the nostalgic sea of retro gaming handhelds. Now, before we get started, do we all have our snacks? Good. Let's get this party started!

1. Game Boy

Ah, the Game Boy! The granddaddy of them all. This little gray brick with its monochrome screen might look like a relic from the stone age to some, but let me tell you something: there is nothing quite like the thrill of catching a pixelated Charizard on this bad boy. Plus, remember the feeling of accomplishment when you finally managed to play it in the dark, using that weird bendy light thingy? Good times.

2. Game Boy Color

Who needs a Game Boy when you can have a Game Boy Color? It's like the Game Boy, but, you know, in color. This handheld was my constant companion during many a boring family road trip. Let's just say, hours flew by as I tried to beat the high score in “Tetris DX”.

3. Game Boy Advance

Well, if the Game Boy Color was a step up, the Game Boy Advance was like taking an escalator to the moon. With its wide screen and comfy design, the GBA looked more like a spaceship control panel than a gaming device. This machine still gives me the chills every time I play "Super Mario Advance".

4. Nintendo DS

Double the screen, double the fun. When the Nintendo DS hit the market, it felt like we'd stepped into the future. I mean, who knew you could design a game to be played on two screens? And can we take a moment to appreciate the stylus? Talk about sophistication, my friends!

5. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Sony's PlayStation Portable, better known as PSP, was like a pocket-sized PS2. This was the handheld that introduced many of us to truly mobile, high-quality 3D gaming. I don’t know about you, but “God of War: Chains of Olympus” on the bus ride home changed my life.

6. Sega Game Gear

Whoa, a Sega handheld on this list? You bet! The Game Gear might have been a battery guzzler (6 AA batteries for 3 hours of playtime? Seriously?), but its backlit color screen and impressive game library made it worth every penny... and hour spent recharging.

7. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Let's talk about the underdog of retro handheld consoles: the Neo Geo Pocket Color. This little gem had a clicky joystick and some killer fighting games. Did it beat the Game Boy? No. But did it offer a unique and fun gaming experience? Absolutely.

8. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was a technological marvel in its time. Sure, it might have been the size of a microwave oven (slight exaggeration), but it was the first handheld with a color LCD and the ability to network with up to 17 other units. It was also ambidextrous. Yes, you heard it right, lefties! This one's for you!

9. WonderSwan

Many might not have heard of the WonderSwan, but it was Bandai's attempt to take on the Game Boy. The design was innovative, and the hardware was powerful. It even had a version of "Final Fantasy" that looked fantastic. Do you WonderSwan? Because, in hindsight, we all should have.

10. Nintendo Switch Lite

Last, but certainly not least, we have the Nintendo Switch Lite. Now I know, some of you might be thinking, “Isn't this a bit too recent to be retro?” Well, my friends, in the fast-paced world of gaming, yesterday is retro. And with the plethora of classic games available from the Nintendo e-shop, the Switch Lite is a retro gaming powerhouse in a sleek, modern package.

And there you have it. 10 retro handheld gaming consoles that, despite advances in technology, continue to provide hours of fun and nostalgia. As we plunge forward into the world of virtual reality and cloud gaming, let's not forget these compact and sturdy pillars of our gaming past. After all, isn't there something magical about holding an entire world of gaming in your hands?
