The Ultimate Guide to Retro Gaming Handhelds: Top Models, Game Tips, and Where to Buy Them

Ah, the good ol’ days of gaming. Remember when you didn't need a graphics card that costs more than a small island, or a monitor with more pixels than the human eye can see? When the only DLC was your mate's saved game on his memory card? Let's take a walk down memory lane, shall we?

A Brief History of Retro Gaming Handhelds

The first handheld gaming device was the Microvision, released in 1979. It was a little chunky, but hey, so were the 70s. Then, Nintendo came along and blew everyone away with the Game Boy in 1989. Yes, folks, it was that long ago. Feel old yet?

The Game Boy was a game-changer (pun intended), and it set the standard for handheld gaming. It was portable, durable, and had a battery life that could last through a long family road trip. Plus, it had some of the best games ever made, like Tetris and Pokémon.

Top Models

Now, let's talk about some of the top models in retro gaming handhelds.

Game Boy - The OG of handheld gaming. It wasn't just a device; it was a cultural phenomenon.

Game Boy Color - The Game Boy's younger, cooler sibling with a color screen. It was like seeing the world in Technicolor for the first time.

Game Boy Advance - The last of the Game Boy line. It had a wide screen and could play all your favorite Game Boy and Game Boy Color games. It was the perfect swan song for a beloved console.

Sega Game Gear - Sega's answer to the Game Boy. It was bigger, heavier, and ate batteries like candy, but it had a backlit color screen. It was like gaming in the future.

Nintendo DS - The Game Boy's successor. It had two screens, a touch screen, and a built-in microphone. It was like a tiny laptop for gaming.

PSP (PlayStation Portable) - Sony's entry into the handheld market. It was sleek, powerful, and played discs. It was the handheld for the hardcore gamers.

Game Tips

Alright, let’s get to the juicy part. Here are a few game tips for some of the most popular retro games:

Tetris - Always leave a gap on one side for the long, straight blocks. Trust me, they're more precious than gold in this game.

Pokémon - Catch 'em all? Nah, just catch the good ones. And remember, type matchups are key. Water beats fire, fire beats grass, grass beats water. It's like a very violent game of rock-paper-scissors.

Super Mario Bros. - Always take the top route in the levels. There's less danger and more coins. And who doesn't love coins?

Sonic the Hedgehog - Speed is good, but control is better. Don't rush through the levels; take your time and collect those rings.

Where to Buy Them

Now, you might be asking, "Where can I buy these wonderful pieces of nostalgia?" Well, there are a few places to check:

eBay - The online yard sale. You can find everything from mint condition consoles to "for parts" junkers.

Craigslist - The local classifieds. Be sure to meet in a public place and check the item thoroughly before buying.

Thrift Stores - These are like treasure hunts. You never know what you'll find, and the prices can be unbeatable.

Retro Gaming Stores - These are becoming more common, and they usually have a good selection of consoles and games.

Conclusion

Retro gaming handhelds are a blast from the past. They remind us of simpler times when games were about fun, not achievements or leaderboards. So why not pick one up, blow off the dust, and lose yourself in some pixelated nostalgia? After all, old is gold, right?
