Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Uncover the Best Classics of All Time

Oh, what a time to be alive. We live in a world where you can hold a device in the palm of your hand with more power than the computers that put a man on the moon. And what do we do with this power? We play games, of course! But let's take a step back, a leap into the past, if you will, and revisit the classics that started it all: retro gaming handhelds.

1. Game Boy

Ah, the Nintendo Game Boy. This bad boy started it all. Tetris, anyone? With its 8-bit processor and 2.6-inch screen, it was the epitome of high-tech in the late 80s. And its monochrome palette? A language of simplicity that spoke volumes. Remember the feeling of the plastic buttons under your thumbs? The thrill of catching your first Pokemon? If you didn't own a Game Boy, did you even have a childhood?

2. Sega Game Gear

Ah, the Sega Game Gear, a handheld console that showed us even Sega could fit in our pockets. Color screen, anyone? This seemed like a luxury compared to the Game Boy. And who could forget the short battery life? Oh, the joy of asking your parents for new batteries every few hours!

3. Atari Lynx

Now, this is where it gets interesting. The Atari Lynx, a color handheld that could be used by both left and right-handed players. Revolutionary, right? It was also the first handheld with a backlight, allowing us to play well past our bedtimes (not that we did, mom and dad).

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color, a handheld where the games were as big as the name. It had a satisfying joystick and a respectable library of games. Maybe not as popular as the Game Boy or Sega Game Gear, but it surely deserves a place in our hearts (and on this list).

5. Nintendo Game Boy Advance

The sequel to our childhood, the Game Boy Advance. With a wider screen and more buttons, it was like the Game Boy had hit puberty. This was also the time when our favorite games started to get fancy with 32-bit graphics. Who could forget the joy of playing 'Advance Wars' or 'Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire'?

6. Sony PSP

The Sony PSP, or as I like to call it, the handheld that thought it was a console. High-quality graphics, a slick design, and a UMD drive (remember those?) for games and movies. It also introduced us to the joys of analog stick control.

7. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS, the handheld console that had us all seeing double. Two screens? Check. Touch input? Check. Microphone for voice commands? Check. This was a handheld that was not afraid to take risks.

8. Game Boy Color

Let's have a moment of silence for the Game Boy Color. This was the Game Boy's cooler, younger sibling that brought our favorite games to life with a burst of color.

9. Sega Nomad

The Sega Nomad, a handheld that allowed you to play your favorite Genesis games on the go. And to think, we thought the Game Gear was Sega's last handheld.

10. Turbo Express

And finally, the Turbo Express. This beast was essentially a handheld version of the TurboGrafx-16 console, complete with a TV tuner. Because who didn't want to watch TV on a 2.6-inch screen?

So there you have it, folks, the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that defined our childhoods (and emptied our parents' wallets). These classics remind us of simpler times, when gaming was about fun and creativity, not just graphics and frame rates. So, here's to the classics, may they live on in our hearts (and our attics) forever.
