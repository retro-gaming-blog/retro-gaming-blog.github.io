Unlock the Nostalgia: Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Must Own in 2023

Buckle up, fellow retro gamers, because we're about to take a wild ride back to the good old days. Remember when our biggest concern was getting past that infuriatingly difficult level in Super Mario Bros? Or when the most stressful part of your day was running out of batteries on your Game Boy during a long car ride? Ah, those were the days.

1. Nintendo Game Boy

First up, the granddaddy of them all, the Nintendo Game Boy. This little grey box was the equivalent of carrying around a portable arcade in your pocket. Tetris, anyone? Mario? Zelda? You name it, the Game Boy had it. And who can forget the iconic "ding" sound when you turned it on? Pure nostalgia.

2. Sega Game Gear

Next, we have the Sega Game Gear. This was Sega's answer to the Game Boy, and boy, did it deliver. Full-color screen, backlit display, and a range of games that would make any Sega fan drool. Sonic on the go? Yes, please!

3. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was a beast of a handheld. It was the first handheld with a color LCD and the ability to link up with other units for multiplayer gaming. Remember when we thought that was mind-blowing? Oh, how far we've come.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the underdog of the retro gaming world. It might not have had the library of games that Game Boy or Game Gear had, but the ones it did have were top-notch. Plus, it had a clicky joystick. A joystick, people!

5. Nintendo Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance was like the Game Boy's cooler, more sophisticated older brother. It had a wider screen, better graphics, and a ton of amazing games. Remember playing Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire on this bad boy? Good times.

6. Sony PlayStation Portable (PSP)

The Sony PSP was a game-changer. Literally. It was like having a PlayStation 2 in your pocket. Grand Theft Auto, God of War, Final Fantasy â€“ all on a handheld. And it played movies! It was like a mini entertainment system.

7. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was the Swiss Army knife of handhelds. Two screens, touchscreen capability, a built-in microphone, and Wi-Fi connectivity. It was like the future in the palm of your hands. Plus, it gave us the best Mario Kart game to date. Fight me if you disagree.

8. Game Boy Color

The Game Boy Color was the Game Boy's trendy cousin. It was smaller, had a color screen, and played all the original Game Boy games. Plus, it came in a range of cool colors. I had the purple one. What about you?

9. Sega Nomad

The Sega Nomad was essentially a portable Sega Genesis. You could play all your favorite Genesis games on the go. It was like a dream come true for Sega fans. The only downside? It ate batteries like Pac-Man eats dots.

10. Nintendo Switch Lite

Last but not least, we have the Nintendo Switch Lite. I know, I know, it's not exactly "retro," but hear me out. It plays all the great Switch games, and it's portable. Plus, it's bringing back some of our favorite retro games in new and exciting ways. It's like the best of both worlds.

So there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds you must own in 2023. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have some old high scores to beat. Game on!
