Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unleashing Nostalgia and FAQs in 2023

Remember those good old days when you could just sit on your bean bag, munching on some chips, and playing your favorite game on your handheld console? Ah, nostalgia! Well, let's take a stroll down memory lane and revive those moments. Buckle up, folks! Because we're about to take a trip to Retroland with the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that'll make you say, "Why did I ever give these up?"

1. Game Boy Color

Did you really think we would start this list without mentioning the godfather of handheld gaming? For many of us, the Game Boy Color was our first foray into the world of handheld gaming. Pokemon, Super Mario, The Legend of Zelda...oh, the memories! Wasn't it just the best feeling to finally catch that elusive Pikachu after hours of relentless hunting? And who could forget those epic Mario Kart battles with friends?

2. Sega Game Gear

"But why stick to one console when you can have two?" said every hardcore gamer ever. Your parents thought otherwise? Well, they just didn't understand. The Sega Game Gear was the Game Boy's fiercer, color-screened counterpart. Sure, it ate batteries faster than you could say "Sonic the Hedgehog," but who cared? It was worth every penny...and AA battery.

3. Atari Lynx

What? You thought Atari was just about the 2600 console? Ha! The Atari Lynx was every bit as cool as its console sibling. It was the first gaming handheld with a color screen and the ability to connect with up to 17 other units for multiplayer action. Sure, it was the size of a small cat, but who cares when you're having fun, right?

4. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was like that perfect sequel that outdid the original. It took everything good about the Game Boy and added a cherry on top with its dual-screen and touch capabilities. Remember feeling like a wizard, casting spells with your stylus in Harry Potter games? Those were the days!

5. Neo Geo Pocket Color

SNK's Neo Geo Pocket Color may not have had the same widespread popularity as the Game Boy or DS, but boy, was it a gem! With its joystick control and a decent library of games, it was a favorite among the gaming community. Or was it just me?

6. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

"What's a gaming list without some Sony love?" said every PlayStation fan ever. The PSP was Sony's first venture into the handheld gaming world and, boy, did it make an entrance. With its impressive graphics and multimedia capabilities, it was a beast of a handheld.

7. Game Boy Advance SP

Backlit screen? Check. Compact design? Check. A boatload of great games? Double-check. The Game Boy Advance SP was the handheld that had it all. It was like that perfect sandwich - everything you loved about handheld gaming, all in one compact package.

8. Nintendo 3DS

Just when we thought Nintendo couldn't outdo itself, it gave us the 3DS. 3D gaming without glasses? Yes, please! It's like they were reading our minds or something.

9. Sega Nomad

What if you could take your Sega Genesis games on the go? That's exactly what the Nomad allowed. It was a portable Genesis! Sure, it was as big as a brick, but it was a small price to pay for portable Sonic goodness.

10. TurboExpress

And finally, we have the TurboExpress - the Rolls Royce of handheld gaming in the '90s. It was basically a portable TurboGrafx-16, complete with the ability to play the same HuCard games. Expensive? Yes. Worth it? Absolutely.

FAQs

Now that we've taken a trip down memory lane, let's tackle some FAQs:

Q: Are these handhelds still available for purchase today?

A: With some luck and persistence, you can find some of these gems in retro gaming stores, online marketplaces, and even garage sales. Happy hunting!

Q: Can I play modern games on these retro handhelds?

A: Now, wouldn't that be something? Unfortunately, these systems were designed for specific games of their time. But hey, that's what makes them retro, right?

Q: Are there any modern handhelds that emulate these retro systems?

A: You bet! There are several options available now like the Retroid Pocket 2 and Anbernic devices that can emulate many of these systems. But remember, nothing beats the real thing!

So there you have it, folks. A trip down memory lane with the top 10 retro gaming handhelds. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have a date with my Game Boy Color and some Pokemon to catch. What are your favorite retro handhelds? Let's keep the nostalgia train rolling in the comments!
