Unleashing Nostalgia: A Comprehensive Guide to Retro Gaming Handhelds - What You Need to Know

If you're reading this, then chances are you're a sucker for nostalgia like me. Ah, the good old days when gaming was in 2D, funky music blasted from poor quality speakers, and you had to blow into the cartridge to make it work. Those were the days!

So, let's take a trip down memory lane and dive into the world of retro gaming handhelds - those chunky, battery-guzzling gadgets that kept us entertained on long car rides.

When the magic began - The early days

Remember the excitement when you first held the Game Boy? The joy of playing 'Super Mario Land' or 'The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening'? The frustration of trying to complete 'Tetris' on the highest difficulty? (What do you mean you've never done that? Am I the only one?).

The Game Boy, released in 1989, was Nintendo's first entry into handheld gaming, and boy did it take the world by storm. Besides the monochrome graphics and 8-bit music, one of the things I remember fondly is the sheer number of batteries this beast required. I'm pretty sure I kept Duracell in business throughout the '90s.

Sega's valiant response - The Game Gear

While Nintendo was having a ball, Sega wasn't about to be left in the dust. Enter the Game Gear, Sega's answer to the Game Boy. The Game Gear boasted a color screen - a game changer back in the day (see what I did there?). Unfortunately, it also boasted a voracious appetite for batteries. Seriously, this thing ate through batteries like I do a bag of potato chips. Still, the sacrifices we made for Sonic, right?

Going 16-bit - The Atari Lynx

Remember when going from 8-bit to 16-bit was like going from VHS to DVD? The Atari Lynx was a beast of a machine, the first handheld console with a color screen and capable of 16-bit graphics. The games were great and graphics stunning for the day. But, like a cruel twist of fate, it was released just weeks before the Game Boy. Talk about bad timing!

The one that time forgot - The Neo Geo Pocket Color

Released in 1999, the Neo Geo Pocket Color (NGPC) had a short lifespan, but for those who were lucky enough to get their hands on one, it was a gem. Boasting a small, compact design and a decent library of games, the NGPC was a formidable competitor. However, it suffered from the "Nintendo curse." Despite its merits, it could never quite outshine the Game Boy.

Handhelds today - The Retro Revival

Fast forward to today, and we're in the midst of a retro revival. From the Nintendo DS to Sony's PSP, modern handhelds have taken the baton from their classic predecessors and carried on the tradition of portable gaming fun.

But what if you're like me and yearn for the simplicity and charm of retro gaming? Well, you're in luck. There's a growing market for retro handhelds, like the Anbernic RG350 or the Bittboy PocketGo, that can emulate old school games. Now, you can play your favorite Game Boy, Game Gear, and even Atari Lynx games on the go.

As we wrap up, I do have to ask, why are we drawn to these old clunky devices? Is it the nostalgia? The simplicity? The lack of microtransactions? Or perhaps it's the memories they evoke, of simpler times when the biggest decision was choosing Bulbasaur, Charmander, or Squirtle (the correct choice is always Charmander, by the way).

Whatever the reason, retro gaming handhelds hold a special place in our hearts. They take us back to a time when gaming was about fun, creativity, and pushing the boundaries of what was possible. And isn't that what video games should be about?
