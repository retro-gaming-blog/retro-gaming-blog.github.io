Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unleashing Nostalgia and Value in 2023

Hey there, gamers, collectors, and anyone who's just about had enough of the latest face-melting, ultra-high-definition, virtual-reality, holodeck-enabled gaming experiences. Sometimes, don't you just want to kick back with some 8-bit glory, a handful of pixels, and a good old fashioned button mashing?

You're not alone! For those of you aching to relive the nostalgic days of yesteryears or perhaps discover the humble beginnings of gaming, join me as we time travel through the top 10 retro gaming handhelds. And remember, no shade thrown towards our modern gaming marvels, love them as we do. We just feel the need to get a little old school occasionally. Heck, maybe we'll even uncover some hidden gems that may have slipped under your radar. Ready? Let's dive in!

1. The Almighty Game Boy

Ah, the Game Boy! Our pixelated gateway into Mushroom Kingdom. Remember when you first laid hands on that chunky piece of gray plastic? The chunky buttons, that pea-green screen, and of course, the hours spent trying to knock out Tyson in 'Punch-Out!!'. Who needs 60 frames per second when you have your trusty Game Boy and a fresh pack of AA batteries, right?

2. Game Boy Color

No, not just a Game Boy with a palette swap! Remember when we were blown away by the sheer idea of playing Pokémon in color? The Game Boy Color brought a fresh splash of vibrancy to our pocket gaming. Plus, it gave us Pokémon Crystal, which is just... chef's kiss.

3. Game Gear

Sega took one look at the Game Boy and said, "Let's do it, but with a backlit screen and a landscape orientation". And wham-bam, Game Gear was born! So what if it drained batteries faster than I drain my coffee cup on Monday mornings? The experience was worth every Energizer.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

SNK's Neo Geo Pocket Color was the underdog that could've been a legend. Touting a strong lineup of games and a slick design, it still fell victim to the Game Boy Color's dominance. But remember Samurai Shodown II and King of Fighters R-2? Yeah, they were just as good as you remember.

5. Atari Lynx

Here's a toast to the world's first handheld with a color display, the Atari Lynx! Sure, it was roughly the size of a skateboard and ergonomics was just a fancy word back then, but it was the 80s and the Lynx was a beautiful beast!

6. Nintendo DS

Ah, the DS, or as I like to call it, the "how many different ways can we fold this thing" console. With its dual screens and stylus-based gameplay, the DS was revolutionary and a game changer. And who can forget the agonizing choice between Nintendogs breeds?

7. Sega Nomad

The Sega Nomad, aka the "let's just stick a whole Genesis in there", was ahead of its time. It was a portable Genesis! You could, and we certainly did, play Streets of Rage during a mundane family road trip. Mom and dad got quiet drives, we got gaming. Win-win, right?

8. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Oh, the PSP, Sony's sleek, sexy foray into handheld territory. Who else watched 'Spiderman 2' on that tiny UMD disc more times than they care to admit? Plus, it had some fantastic games like God of War: Chains of Olympus, Lumines, and the entire Final Fantasy series.

9. WonderSwan Color

The WonderSwan Color, a.k.a. the "Game Boy Color, but kind of diagonal". This little gem from Bandai never made it out of Japan, but it's worth seeking out for its unique form factor and excellent Final Fantasy ports.

10. Game Boy Advance SP

Ending where we started with another Game Boy, namely the Game Boy Advance SP. It was everything we loved about the original and more. The illuminated screen alone meant no more sneaking the desk lamp under your blanket for those late-night gaming marathons.

And there you have it, fellow time travelers, the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that continue to charm us in 2023. Whether you're a retro gaming vet or a curious newcomer, these pieces of hand-held history offer countless hours of button mashing goodness.

Here's to the pixelated past, the high-definition present, and the untold future of gaming. May our thumbs always be nimble, our batteries ever charged, and our games forever exciting. Game on, folks!
