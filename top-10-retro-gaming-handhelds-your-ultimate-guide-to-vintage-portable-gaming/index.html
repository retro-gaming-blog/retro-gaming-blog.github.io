Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Your Ultimate Guide to Vintage Portable Gaming

Ah, the good old days of gaming, when graphics were made up of pixelated blobs, sound effects were more bleep-bloop than Dolby Atmos, and a handheld was as big as a brick. But boy, did we love it! Those were the days when gaming was pure and simple, devoid of any loot boxes, DLCs, or microtransactions. If you're a gamer who still yearns for those simpler times, or if you're a newbie looking to explore the pixelated past of your favorite pastime, this post is for you. So, strap in, put on your nostalgia goggles, and let's take a trip down memory lane with the top 10 retro gaming handhelds of all time.

1. Game Boy

I mean, come on! Were you really expecting anything else at number one? The Game Boy is the OG, the big kahuna, the...you get the idea. Released by Nintendo in 1989, this little grey brick with its pea-green screen took the world by storm. Who can forget the countless hours spent trying to save the princess in Super Mario Land or the feeling of satisfaction when you finally caught 'em all in Pokémon Red and Blue? A moment of silence for all the AA batteries we lost though.

2. Atari Lynx

What? Atari made a handheld? Yes, they did, and it was pretty darn good too. Released in 1989, the Atari Lynx was way ahead of its time. It had a color screen when Game Boy was still stuck in the monochrome era. Sadly, it suffered from a high price tag and a lack of killer games, but it still holds a special place in the hearts of many retro gamers.

3. Sega Game Gear

Ah, the Sega Game Gear, the handheld that could've been a contender. Released in 1990, it was technically superior to the Game Boy with a full-color screen and a landscape orientation. But its battery life was shorter than a goldfish's attention span. Despite that, it had some great games like Sonic the Hedgehog and Columns, making it a worthy addition to any retro gaming collection.

4. Nintendo Game Boy Advance

Now, this is a handheld that needs no introduction. The Game Boy Advance, or GBA as it's affectionately known, took everything that was great about the original Game Boy and dialed it up to 11. It had a wider screen, better graphics, and a fantastic library of games. And let's not forget the SP version with its rechargeable battery and front-lit screen. Pure genius!

5. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the handheld that could've, should've but sadly, didn't. Released by SNK in 1999, it was a powerhouse with a fantastic joystick and a great line-up of fighting games. But, it was up against the GBA and we all know how that turned out. It still deserves a spot on this list for its sheer audacity to take on the big boys.

6. Game Boy Color

The Game Boy Color was the handheld we didn't know we needed until we got it. It brought color to the Game Boy line-up and gave us some fantastic games like The Legend of Zelda: Oracle of Seasons and Pokémon Gold and Silver. It may have been a stop-gap before the GBA, but it still holds a special place in our hearts.

7. Tiger Electronics Handheld Games

Okay, okay, I can hear you screaming, "These aren't real gaming handhelds!" And to that, I say, "Oh, come on!" Sure, they were single game, LCD devices with crude graphics and sound, but they were a staple of 90s childhood. Plus, who can forget the thrill of finally beating that unbeatable level on your favorite Tiger game?

8. Sega Nomad

Think of the Sega Nomad as the Game Gear's beefed-up big brother. It could play full-size Sega Genesis cartridges, which was mind-blowing at the time. Unfortunately, it ate through batteries like a gamer through a bag of Doritos, and it was pretty expensive. But for the ability to play Sonic on the go, it was worth every penny.

9. TurboExpress

TurboExpress was basically the TurboGrafx-16 in a portable form. This little gem from NEC had a huge screen by 1990s standards and could play all TurboGrafx-16 games. Unfortunately, like many on this list, it suffered from a high price tag and battery issues. But, it's still a neat little piece of gaming history.

10. Nintendo DS

Last but not least, we have the Nintendo DS. Yes, it's more modern than the others on this list, but it's hard to deny its impact. With its two screens, touchscreen capability, and iconic games like New Super Mario Bros and Nintendogs, it revolutionized handheld gaming.

So, there you have it folks, the top 10 retro gaming handhelds of all time. Whether you're a gaming old-timer or a newbie, these handhelds offer a glimpse into the history of portable gaming. And who knows, maybe you'll find your new favorite game in one of these classic devices. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have a high score to beat on Tetris. Game on!
