Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Uncover the Nostalgia and Discover Where to Buy Them

Oh, the '80s and '90s - a time when fashion was questionable, music was on vinyl, and gaming was, well, not what it is today. But there's no denying the charm and nostalgia of retro gaming handhelds. These little gadgets are a true blast from the past, transporting us back to a simpler time when all we needed to be entertained was a couple of AA batteries and a pocket-sized electronic device. So join me as we count down the top 10 retro gaming handhelds, and yes, they're still available for purchase if you know where to look.

10. Tiger Electronics Handhelds

Remember these? Tiger Electronics handhelds were the epitome of '90s gaming. They were simple, they were cheap, and they were everywhere. Each device featured a single game with basic controls and graphics. They weren't sophisticated, but they sure were fun.

9. Game Boy Color

Game Boy Color, oh what a revolutionary device you were. You brought us portable gaming in color, an upgrade that had us all flabbergasted. The array of games available made this a must-have for any '90s kid. Pok√©mon Yellow, anyone?

8. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the underdog in the handheld gaming world, overshadowed by the mighty Game Boy. But those who were lucky enough to have one know that it was a gem. With its joystick control and vibrant graphics, this little console was ahead of its time.

7. Sega Game Gear

Sega Game Gear, the alternative to Game Boy, boasted a backlit screen and a decent library of games. Although it was a battery guzzler, it was worth every Duracell for the chance to play Sonic the Hedgehog on the go.

6. Atari Lynx

Atari Lynx, the world's first handheld with color LCD, had the potential to be great. Despite its innovative features like network gaming, it couldn't compete with the battery life and affordability of the Game Boy. But let's take a moment to appreciate its ambition.

5. TurboExpress

TurboExpress, a handheld version of the TurboGrafx-16 console, was a true luxury item in the early '90s. It had a high price tag, but it offered the ability to play actual console games on the go. Talk about impressive!

4. Game Boy Advance

Game Boy Advance, the big brother of the Game Boy Color, was a huge leap forward in handheld gaming. With a wider screen and improved graphics, it brought console-quality games to our pockets. And who can forget the joy of playing Super Mario Advance for the first time?

3. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was a game changer (pun intended). With its dual screen, touch controls, and impressive library of games, it was a true innovator in the handheld gaming world. And yes, we all tried to blow the candles out on Cooking Mama.

2. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

The PSP was Sony's first venture into handheld consoles, and boy, did they nail it. With its sleek design and ability to play movies and music, it was more than just a gaming device. It was a multimedia powerhouse.

1. Game Boy

Ah, the original Game Boy. Despite its monochrome screen and chunky design, it was a revolutionary device that brought portable gaming into the mainstream. With classics like Tetris and Super Mario Land, it holds a special place in every retro gamer's heart.

So there you have it, folks. Ten blasts from the past that kept us entertained on long car rides, during boring school breaks, and well, pretty much every spare moment we had. For those looking to buy, most of these retro gaming handhelds can be found on eBay, Amazon, and various retro gaming websites. Just remember to check the condition and make sure they're working before you buy. After all, it's all fun and games until your Game Boy doesn't power on.

Whether you're a collector or just feeling nostalgic, these handhelds offer a trip down memory lane. So go ahead, indulge in some retro gaming. Happy gaming, folks!
