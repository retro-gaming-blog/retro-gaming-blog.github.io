Ultimate Guide to Retro Gaming Handhelds: Discover Top Models, Hidden Gems, And Tips for Buyers

Hello, fellow gaming enthusiasts, and welcome to my ultimate guide to retro gaming handhelds! Grab your pixelated popcorn because we're about to embark on a nostalgia-fueled journey back to the golden age of gaming. You know, back when we were blowing into cartridges instead of swiping on screens.

Top Models to Consider

Game Boy

Let's start with the granddaddy of them all, the original Nintendo Game Boy. This bad boy was the epitome of cool back in the day. It was like having an arcade in your pocket, which was a massive deal in a world where the internet was still a pipe dream. You remember those days, right? When we actually had to go somewhere to play video games?

Game Boy was the first to make gaming portable and boy, did we love it for that. With its monochrome screen and iconic games like Super Mario Land and The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening, this handheld device was a game-changer (pun very much intended).

Game Gear

Next up, we have the Sega Game Gear. This was Sega's answer to the Game Boy. It was like the hip, rebellious cousin who came to family gatherings in a leather jacket. The Game Gear boasted a color screen, which was like seeing the world in technicolor after The Wizard of Oz. It was a whole new world, folks.

Atari Lynx

And let's not forget about the Atari Lynx. This was the first handheld with a color screen, but it didn't quite take off like the Game Boy or Game Gear. But hey, not everyone can be a Beatles or an Elvis, right? Some of us are just destined to be the One-Hit Wonders of the world.

Hidden Gems

Now that we've covered the obvious choices, let's dig a little deeper and unearth some hidden gems.

Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was like that indie band that only the cool kids knew about. It had some killer games like Samurai Shodown and King of Fighters R-2, but it was criminally overlooked at the time. It deserves a second look, though, if you're into retro gaming.

WonderSwan

Then there's the WonderSwan. This little device was only released in Japan, which gives it an air of mystery and exclusivity. It's like the secret menu at In-N-Out Burger. If you're a true retro gaming connoisseur, you'll want to get your hands on one of these.

Tips for Buyers

Before you rush off to eBay to start bidding on these old-school treasures, let's go over a few tips for buying retro gaming handhelds.

  1. Check the condition: This might seem obvious, but it's easy to get caught up in the excitement and overlook the details. Make sure the device is in good working condition. Ask for pictures, videos, anything that proves it's not just a fancy paperweight.

  2. Beware of fakes: Yes, there are counterfeit retro gaming handhelds out there. It's a cruel world, my friends. If the price seems too good to be true, it probably is.

  3. Consider the games: The device is important, but the games are what really matter. Check what games come with the handheld and if they're ones you actually want to play.

  4. Don't forget the power source: Some of these old devices run on batteries that could power a small village. Make sure you're prepared for that.

And there you have it, folks! Your ultimate guide to retro gaming handhelds. Whether you're a long-time lover of all things retro or a newbie just dipping your toes in the pixelated waters, I hope this guide has been helpful. Now go forth and game on!
