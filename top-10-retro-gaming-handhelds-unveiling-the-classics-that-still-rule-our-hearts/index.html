Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Classics that Still Rule Our Hearts

Ah, retro gaming handhelds. Brings back memories, doesn't it? Days spent engrossed in pixelated adventures, saving princesses, battling monsters, and racing cars. The eighties and nineties were a golden era for gaming, and one that will always have a special place in our hearts. But what were the top 10 retro handheld gaming consoles that have etched themselves in our memories? Let's take a trip down memory lane and find out.

1. Game Boy - Nintendo

Now, did you honestly think we'd start this list with anything other than the Game Boy? C'mon now. This bad boy was the king of handhelds, the big cheese, the pièce de résistance. With its rich library of games, including the iconic 'Super Mario Land' and 'Pokémon', the Game Boy had us hooked from the get-go. Plus, its long battery life meant fewer interruptions in our quest to catch 'em all. Not to mention, this device survived a bombing during the Gulf War. Talk about durability!

2. Game Gear - Sega

Alright, Sega fans, don't get your knickers in a twist. We haven't forgotten about the Game Gear. This handheld was Sega's answer to Nintendo's Game Boy, and boy, did it deliver! With a backlit color display (take that, Game Boy), the Game Gear felt like a blast from the future. Sonic the Hedgehog on a portable device? Yes, please!

3. Atari Lynx

This one's a bit of a dark horse. The Atari Lynx was the first handheld console with a color display - a fact that's often overshadowed by the Game Gear. It's like being the oldest sibling; you do everything first, but no one remembers. Regardless, with its ambidextrous layout and impressive (for the time) 3D graphics, the Lynx deserves a spot on this list.

4. Nintendo Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance, or GBA for the cool kids, took what made the original Game Boy great and made it even better. It was like when your favorite movie got a sequel that was actually good. With a horizontal design and enhanced graphics, the GBA paved the way for handheld gaming as we know it today.

5. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Who remembers the Neo Geo Pocket Color? Anyone? No? Just me then. Despite being a commercial flop, this handheld was a gem. With a joystick for controls and a decent library of games, the Neo Geo Pocket Color was like that indie band that's super good but no one's heard of.

6. Bandai Wonderswan

The Wonderswan, brought to you by the same folks who created the Game Boy, was a Japan-exclusive handheld. So, unless you were on the other side of the Pacific, you probably missed out. And miss out you did. With a design that allowed for both vertical and horizontal gaming, the Wonderswan was turning heads and taking names.

7. Sega Nomad

Remember that cousin who had all the cool toys? The Sega Nomad was that cousin. This handheld could play Genesis games. Yes, you read that right. Genesis games. In your hands. Mic drop.

8. Nintendo DS

Would this even be a list without the Nintendo DS? With its two screens (one of them being a touchscreen. Revolutionary, right?), built-in microphone, and Wi-Fi capabilities, the DS was the gadget to have.

9. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Sony's PSP was the black sheep of the handheld family - in a good way. With its wide screen, insane graphics, and multimedia capabilities, the PSP was like a handheld PS2. If you didn't have one of these, you were seriously missing out.

10. Game Boy Color

Last, but certainly not least, the Game Boy Color. This was Nintendo's answer to the color display of its competitors, and what an answer it was! With its vibrant display, compact design, and an impressive roster of games, the Game Boy Color was the icing on the cake of Nintendo's handheld dominance.

And there you have it, folks! The top 10 retro gaming handhelds that still rule our hearts. These handhelds defined a generation and set the stage for the gaming landscape we know today. Who knows, maybe one day, the Nintendo Switch and PlayStation Vita will join this list of classics. But for now, they can wait their turn in line.
