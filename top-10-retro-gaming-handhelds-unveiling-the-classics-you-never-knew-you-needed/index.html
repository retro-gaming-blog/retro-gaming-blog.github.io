Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unveiling the Classics You Never Knew You Needed

Ah, nostalgia. The sweet, sweet feeling that makes us yearn for the good old days. Remember when we used to blow on cartridges to make them work? Or when we'd squint at our tiny screens in the dark because backlighting wasn't a thing yet? Those were the days, my friend. But let's dive right in, shall we?

1. Game Boy

Oh, come on. You knew this was coming. It's impossible to talk about retro handheld gaming without mentioning the one, the only, Nintendo Game Boy. This little grey brick was the gateway to magical worlds for many of us. Tetris, anyone? And who could forget the joy of catching your first Pokémon on Pokémon Red or Blue? Ahh, the memories.

2. Game Boy Color

Yes, technically it's still a Game Boy, but it's in color! This was a game-changer, folks. Suddenly, our favorite games were not just in boring old black and white. We had a whole 32,768 colors to play with. It was like going from Kansas to Oz.

3. Game Gear

Remember when Sega was still in the console business? Pepperidge Farm remembers. The Game Gear was Sega's answer to the Game Boy, and boy, did it deliver. With a full color screen and a landscape design, it was ahead of its time. Too bad it ate batteries like Pac-Man eats dots.

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Alright, let's get a little obscure. The Neo Geo Pocket Color was SNK's attempt to break into the handheld market. While it didn't quite reach Game Boy levels of success, it had some pretty cool games. Ever played a little gem called Samurai Shodown? Then you know what I'm talking about.

5. Atari Lynx

Atari. The name alone sends shivers down the spine of any retro gaming enthusiast. The Lynx was their foray into the handheld world. It was the first handheld with color and it was also the first to offer multiplayer gaming. Too bad it was about the size of a small car.

6. TurboExpress

The TurboExpress was basically a portable version of the TurboGrafx-16 console. It even played the same HuCards! It was like having a console in your pocket. If only it didn't cost as much as a console...

7. Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance was the culmination of everything Nintendo learned from the original Game Boy and the Game Boy Color. It had a wider screen, better graphics, and it could even play old Game Boy games. Plus, it introduced us to the world of WarioWare. Enough said.

8. Sega Nomad

The Sega Nomad was a portable Genesis. Yes, you read that right. A portable Genesis. It played all your favorite Genesis cartridges. Streets of Rage 2 on the go? Yes, please!

9. WonderSwan

This little-known handheld from Bandai was only released in Japan. It was designed by Gunpei Yokoi, the man behind the Game Boy. It had some pretty awesome games, including some Final Fantasy titles. Too bad we never got it in the West.

10. Nintendo DS

Alright, I can hear you saying, "But the DS isn't retro!" Well, it's over 15 years old, so it qualifies in my book. Plus, it introduced us to the concept of dual screens and touch controls. Not to mention the countless hours spent on Brain Age.

So, there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds you never knew you needed. So, the next time you're feeling nostalgic, why not dust off that old Game Boy and give Tetris another go? You know you want to.
