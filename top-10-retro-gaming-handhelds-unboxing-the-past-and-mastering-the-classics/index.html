Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unboxing the Past and Mastering the Classics

Remember the good old days? No, I don’t mean when we used to play in the park or ride our bikes, I mean when we were hunched over our gaming handhelds, completely engrossed in catching ‘em all or saving the princess. Remember that? Those were the times, right?

Well, you’re in for a treat because we’re about to take a trip down memory lane. Let’s dive deep into the world of retro gaming handhelds, unbox the past, and master the classics.

The Atari Lynx

We're kicking off with the Atari Lynx, a device ahead of its time, bless its heart. The Lyxn came with color graphics — quite the wow factor back in 1989, believe me. It was like having an arcade right in your backpack. Sadly, it was the size and weight of an actual arcade, and the battery life was about as long as a TikTok video. But hey, you can’t have everything.

Sega Game Gear

Next up, Sega's answer to Nintendo's Game Boy — the Game Gear! This beautiful creature ate six AA batteries for breakfast, lunch, AND dinner. Still, we loved it. With a full-color backlit screen, it was like the Lynx's younger and cooler brother. Its games library was quite impressive, including Shinobi, Sonic, and, my personal favorite, Echo the Dolphin.

Game Boy

Oh boy, where to start with this one? The iconic Nintendo Game Boy — simplistic in its design, monochrome on the screen, yet power-packed with awesome games like Tetris and Pokemon Red & Blue. This little bundle of joy launched many of us into the gaming universe, and we couldn't be more grateful.

Game Boy Color

The Game Boy Color, the vibrant younger sibling to the Game Boy, gave us COLOUR! Can you believe it? The sheer joy of playing classics like Super Mario Bros Deluxe in color was a revelation.

Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance — or GBA if you want to sound cool — was what we all wanted to be when we grew up — sleek, sophisticated, and advanced. Its horizontal layout was a game-changer. Metroid Fusion and Fire Emblem? Yes, please!

Game Boy Advance SP

Basically, the GBA decided to close the lid and call itself the GBA SP. Yet, it was still a revelation because it actually had a rechargeable battery! Revolutionary, right? And it had a front-lit screen. Nighttime gaming had never been so easy.

Game Boy Micro

Ever thought about playing your favorite games on a tiny screen? No? Well, Nintendo thought you might fancy it so they released the Game Boy Micro. The smallest addition in the Game Boy family, it was so small, you could lose it in your pocket. Literally.

Neo Geo Pocket Color

Here's a heavyweight challenger — the Neo Geo Pocket Color. In the right corner, we have the Game Boy Color, and in the left, this bad boy. It boasted an impressive 16-bit graphics processor, but could it dethrone the champ? Spoiler alert: No. But it was still a really great contender.

Nintendo DS

The DS stands for "Developer's System" or "Dual Screen" — I like to think of it as "Darn Sexy." With its clamshell design and touchscreen, the DS was truly a class apart. But it wasn't just a pretty face, the gaming library was a treasure trove of classics.

PlayStation Portable (PSP)

And last, but certainly not least, we have Sony's PlayStation Portable. The PSP was like the suave, sophisticated gentleman in a martini bar, amidst a crowd of beer guzzlers. It brought high-quality graphics and mature titles to the handheld market, and we couldn't have been more thrilled.

And that's all folks! Our wonderful nostalgia-drenched trip is over. But remember, these devices aren't just relics of a bygone era — they're vessels of joy, transporting us back to a simpler time. So which one was your favorite? And more importantly, can you still beat your high score?
