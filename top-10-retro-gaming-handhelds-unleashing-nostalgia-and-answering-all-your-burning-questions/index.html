Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unleashing Nostalgia and Answering All Your Burning Questions

Ah, the good old days. Remember when gaming was all about that sweet, sweet 8-bit glory? When the most advanced thing we could do was jump over a pixelated turtle (I'm looking at you, Mario)? Well, if you're like me and you're feeling a bit nostalgic for those simpler times, then you're in the right place. Welcome to the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that'll transport you back to your childhood faster than a DeLorean going 88 miles per hour.

1. Game Boy (1989)

In the beginning, there was the Game Boy. And it was good. No, scratch that, it was great. With its monochrome screen and chunky buttons, it was the handheld that started it all. Tetris, Super Mario Land, The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening - these were the games that defined a generation. And if you didn't have one, were you even a 90s kid?

2. Game Gear (1990)

Sega's answer to the Game Boy, the Game Gear was like the cool older brother who had a color screen and backlight. Sure, it ate batteries like Pac-Man eats dots, but who cared when you had games like Sonic the Hedgehog and Streets of Rage?

3. Game Boy Color (1998)

The Game Boy Color was basically the Game Boy's younger, cooler sibling. It had a color screen (hence the name), and it could even play original Game Boy games. Talk about versatility!

4. Neo Geo Pocket Color (1999)

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the underdog of the handheld world. It may not have been as popular as the Game Boy or Game Gear, but it had some killer games. Samurai Shodown, anyone?

5. Game Boy Advance (2001)

The Game Boy Advance was like the Game Boy on steroids. It had a wider screen, better graphics, and it could play all the Game Boy and Game Boy Color games. Plus, it had some of the best games ever, like Pok√©mon Ruby and Sapphire, and The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap.

6. Nintendo DS (2004)

The Nintendo DS was a game-changer, literally. Two screens? Touchscreen controls? It was like the future had arrived, and it was handheld. And let's not forget about games like New Super Mario Bros and The Legend of Zelda: Phantom Hourglass.

7. PlayStation Portable (2004)

The PlayStation Portable, or PSP as it's more commonly known, was Sony's first foray into the handheld market. And boy, did they make an entrance. With its sleek design and impressive game library (God of War: Chains of Olympus, anyone?), the PSP proved that handheld gaming wasn't just for kids.

8. Nintendo 3DS (2011)

The Nintendo 3DS was like the DS, but with 3D. And while the 3D may have been a bit of a gimmick, the games were anything but. From Super Mario 3D Land to The Legend of Zelda: A Link Between Worlds, the 3DS had some seriously good games.

9. PlayStation Vita (2011)

The PlayStation Vita was the PSP's successor, and while it may not have been as successful, it was still a pretty awesome handheld. With games like Uncharted: Golden Abyss and Persona 4 Golden, the Vita proved that handheld gaming could be just as epic as console gaming.

10. Nintendo Switch (2017)

Okay, so the Nintendo Switch isn't exactly "retro," but it's too good not to include. With its hybrid design and killer game library (Breath of the Wild, Super Mario Odyssey, etc.), the Switch has taken handheld gaming to new heights.

So there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds that'll make you feel like a kid again. Now, if you'll excuse me, I've got some Goombas to stomp.

Remember, whether you're a Game Boy fan or a PSP devotee, the important thing is that we all share a love for gaming. So let's put aside our differences and unite in our shared nostalgia. Because at the end of the day, isn't that what gaming is all about?
