Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Everything You Need to Know Before Buying

If you're anything like me, there's nothing quite like the nostalgia of playing a classic 8-bit game. Don't get me wrong, I love my PS5 and the lifelike graphics it offers. But sometimes, the simplicity of a pixelated plumber or a round yellow blob eating dots is all I need. Are you with me? Good.

So, you're thinking about buying a retro gaming handheld, huh? Well, you're in luck. I've compiled a list of the top 10. Buckle up, it's gonna be one helluva ride down memory lane.

1. Game Boy

Oh, the Game Boy. The granddaddy of handheld consoles. If you remember green screens and Tetris, you know what I'm talking about. It was the iPod of the early 90s. If you didn't have one, you were a nobody. Sorry, I don't make the rules.

2. Game Boy Color

Did someone say color? Well, more like a limited palette of 56 colors, but hey, it was groundbreaking at the time. The Game Boy Color gave us a whole new dimension to our favorite games. Suddenly, Mario's world wasn't just green and black. What a time to be alive.

3. Game Boy Advance

If the Game Boy was the iPod, then the Game Boy Advance was the iPhone. It was sleek, it was stylish, and it had a backlit screen. Luxury. To this day, it remains one of the best handhelds for retro gaming.

4. Nintendo DS

Who could forget the first time they saw a Nintendo DS? Two screens? A touchscreen? Sorcery. Yet, somehow, it worked. It gave us unique games like Brain Age and Nintendogs. Plus, it had a built-in microphone. Because who doesn't want to shout at their DS in public?

5. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Ah, the PSP. Sony's attempt to muscle in on Nintendo's handheld market. And you know what? It wasn't half bad. The graphics were top-notch, and it had a neat little UMD slot for games and movies. Yes, physical media. Remember those?

6. Sega Game Gear

The Game Gear was Sega's answer to the Game Boy. It was chunky, it was heavy, and it ate batteries like Pac-Man eats dots. But boy, did it have a great color screen. If you can get past its battery-guzzling tendencies, it's a solid choice for retro gamers.

7. Nintendo 3DS

The 3DS is a gift that keeps on giving. Sure, the 3D feature was a bit gimmicky, but it had a great library of games. And let's not forget about StreetPass. Who knew walking past strangers could be so fun?

8. Neo Geo Pocket Color

Ever heard of the Neo Geo Pocket Color? No? Well, you're missing out. It was essentially the hipster of handheld consoles. It wasn't mainstream, but it had a dedicated following. And for good reason. It had some great games, like Samurai Shodown and King of Fighters.

9. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was the first handheld console with a color LCD. It also had ambidextrous controls, which was pretty innovative. Sadly, it didn't catch on. But if you're a retro gaming aficionado, it's worth checking out.

10. WonderSwan

Last but not least, we have the WonderSwan. This little-known handheld was only released in Japan. But if you can get your hands on one, it's a real treat. It was designed by the same guy who created the Game Boy. Need I say more?

So there you have it, folks. The top 10 retro gaming handhelds. Whether you're a die-hard Nintendo fan, a Sega enthusiast, or an indie-loving hipster, there's something for everyone. Just remember, it's not about the graphics or the features. It's about the fun. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have some dots to eat.
