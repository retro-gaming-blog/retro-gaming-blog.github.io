Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Must Try: Unmasking the Classics That Shaped the Gaming World

So, you fancy yourself a gaming connoisseur, huh? You've burned through the latest PS5 titles, conquered the Nintendo Switch landscape, and maybe even dabbled in some PC gaming goodness. But let me ask you something - have you ever ventured into the golden era of handheld gaming?

Before you scoff and say, "Pssht, I've played on my phone," I'm not talking about Candy Crush Saga or Clash of Clans here. I mean the classic, pixel-heavy, battery devouring beasts of yesteryears. Yeah, that's right - I'm taking you back, way back, to the pioneers of handheld gaming. Buckle up, folks, as we unmask the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that shaped the gaming world.

1. Game Boy

Oh, come on, you knew this bad boy was going to be number one. The Nintendo Game Boy is the OG, the big kahuna, the Michael Jordan of gaming handhelds. Tetris, anyone? Pokemon Red and Blue, perhaps? This 8-bit wonder sold over 118 million units and revolutionized the gaming world.

2. Game Boy Color

What do you do when you've already changed the world with one handheld console? You release another, of course! Enter the Game Boy Color. Now you could catch them all in COLOR. Mind blown, right?

3. Game Boy Advance

Yes, another Game Boy, but seriously, could you blame me? The Game Boy Advance was like the big brother who stepped in when the Game Boy Color started feeling a bit dated. This console introduced us to an arsenal of new games, showing us that handhelds could compete with their console counterparts.

4. Sega Game Gear

Ah, the Sega Game Gear, the Game Boy's arch-enemy. This 8-bit behemoth gave us COLOR before the Game Boy Color even existed. Plus, it had a backlit screen! The only downside? It ate batteries like a kid with a sugar rush.

5. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was the little console that could. It was Sega and Nintendo's lesser-known competitor, but that didn't stop it from delivering some top-notch gaming experiences. The fighting games on this console? Chef's kiss.

6. Atari Lynx

Okay, so the Atari Lynx was kind of a flop when it came out, but that doesn't mean it wasn't ahead of its time. It was the first gaming handheld with a color LCD and was designed for both right and left-handed gamers. Respect, Atari. Respect.

7. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was a game-changer, quite literally. With its dual screen (one of them touch-sensitive), microphone, and Wi-Fi capabilities, it was the future of gaming in a clamshell design.

8. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Who could forget the PSP? Sony's first and only foray into handheld gaming was a powerhouse, offering console-quality gaming on the go. It was like having a mini PS2 in your pocket (if you had really, really big pockets).

9. Game & Watch

Before the Game Boy, there was Game & Watch. These little handhelds were simple, with just one game per device and a built-in clock (hence the name), but they were also incredibly fun. They're so retro; they're hipster-level cool.

10. WonderSwan

The WonderSwan was a Japan-only handheld that gave Nintendo a run for its money. It had a unique design that allowed for both portrait and landscape gaming. Plus, it had a Final Fantasy game. Need I say more?

So there you have it, folks - the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that shaped the gaming world. If you've not tried these, you're missing out on a glorious piece of gaming history. And if you have? Well, how about another spin in the nostalgia wheel? After all, old is gold.

Game on, fellow gamers, game on.
