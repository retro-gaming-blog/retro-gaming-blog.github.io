Top 10 Retro Handheld Gaming Systems: Unleashing Nostalgia in 2023

Ah, nostalgia. Isn't it a wonderful thing? It's like that old, comfy sweater you can't bear to part with, even though it has more holes than a piece of Swiss cheese. And when it comes to video games, nostalgia is like that but with pixelated graphics and catchy 8-bit tunes.

So, let's take a journey together, shall we? A journey back to a simpler time when the only thing we had to worry about was whether we had enough AA batteries to keep our Game Boy running. Here are the top 10 retro handheld gaming systems that are sure to unleash a tidal wave of nostalgia in 2023.

1. Game Boy

Oh, the Game Boy. The granddaddy of all handhelds. This little brick of a device was as sturdy as a tank and probably weighed as much too. Remember when you had to sit under a lamp to see the screen? Yeah, those were the days.

2. Game Boy Color

Then came the Game Boy Color, and the world was never the same. Who knew that adding a bit of color could make such a difference? Suddenly, those little pixelated monsters we were catching had a whole new dimension.

3. Game Boy Advance

Just when we thought things couldn't get any better, the Game Boy Advance came onto the scene. With its landscape orientation and shoulder buttons, it felt like we were holding a tiny Super Nintendo in our hands. And let's not forget about the backlight, a feature that was truly revolutionary. No more squinting under a lamp!

4. Sega Game Gear

The Sega Game Gear was always the cool kid on the block. With its color screen and sleek design, it was like the Ferrari of handhelds. Too bad it ate batteries faster than a hungry hippo.

5. Nintendo DS

Who needs one screen when you can have two? The Nintendo DS was a game-changer, literally. With its dual screens and touch capabilities, it opened up a whole new world of gaming possibilities. It was also the first handheld to make us look like we were talking to our games. Remember Nintendogs? Yeah, those puppies were adorable.

6. PlayStation Portable (PSP)

The PSP was Sony's answer to Nintendo's handheld dominance. With its wide screen and impressive graphics, it felt like we were holding a tiny PlayStation 2 in our hands. And let's not forget about the UMDs. Those little discs were as fragile as a snowflake but held so much gaming goodness.

7. Atari Lynx

The Atari Lynx was the handheld that could've been. It was the first handheld with a color screen and the ability to link up with other systems for multiplayer games. Too bad it was as big as a brick and just as heavy.

8. Neo Geo Pocket Color

The Neo Geo Pocket Color was like the little engine that could. It didn't have the same brand recognition as Nintendo or Sega, but it had a solid lineup of games and a joystick that was as smooth as butter.

9. Nintendo Game Boy Micro

The Game Boy Micro was Nintendo's attempt to make the smallest handheld possible, and boy did they succeed. This thing was so small you could lose it in your pocket. But despite its size, it packed a punch with the same great Game Boy Advance games.

10. WonderSwan

Last but not least, we have the WonderSwan. This handheld was only released in Japan, but it had a cult following in the West thanks to its unique design and solid lineup of games. It also had the distinction of being the only handheld that could be played both vertically and horizontally.

So, there you have it. The top 10 retro handheld gaming systems that will surely unleash a wave of nostalgia in 2023. Whether you were a Game Boy kid, a Sega fan, or a PSP devotee, these systems hold a special place in our hearts.

Now, if you'll excuse me, I have some pixelated monsters to catch.
