Unlocking the Nostalgia: Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds You Must Own in 2023

Oh, gaming handhelds. Those little pieces of tech nostalgia that transport us back to the simpler times of our youth. Remember when the biggest worry you had was beating the next level of Super Mario Land on your Gameboy during a long car ride? No bills, no job stress, just pure gaming bliss. Well, buckle up, because we're about to step into the time machine and revisit some of those portable gaming titans of yesteryear.

1. Nintendo Game Boy

We can't talk about retro handhelds without starting with the OG, the Nintendo Game Boy. Classic, iconic, and a little chunky, this little grey brick was the start of so many of our gaming journeys. Tetris, anyone? Of course, it had that glorious green screen that was about as bright as a candle, but who cares? It's 2023, and we're here for the nostalgia, not picture quality!

2. Sega Game Gear

Next up, we're stepping into Sega's ring with the Game Gear. Now, this bad boy was like the sports car of handhelds. Colour screen? Check. Backlit? Check. Able to guzzle six AA batteries in 30 minutes? Double-check. But hey, it was worth every penny.

3. Nintendo Game Boy Color

Ah, the Game Boy Color. It took Nintendo a while to jump on the color bandwagon, but when they did, boy, was it worth the wait. Those epic Pokemon battles never looked better.

4. Atari Lynx

Did someone say, "backlit color screen"? Atari was way ahead of the curve with the Lynx. It was a bit of a flop back in the day, but we retro connoisseurs know it's a must-have in our collections.

5. Neo Geo Pocket Color

We can't forget the Neo Geo Pocket Color. This one's a bit of a hidden gem, but trust me, it's worth hunting down. With a joystick that clicks in eight directions and some killer games like SNK vs. Capcom, it's a win-win.

6. Nintendo Game Boy Advance

The Game Boy Advance or GBA, as the cool kids call it. A bit of an upgrade from the Game Boy Color, and by that, I mean it plays the same games but horizontally. Groundbreaking, Nintendo.

7. Sony PlayStation Portable (PSP)

Sony's PlayStation Portable, the PSP, was a powerhouse of a handheld. It had a massive library of games and could even play movies. Remember UMDs? Yeah, me neither.

8. Nintendo DS

The Nintendo DS was Nintendo's "let's try something different" phase. Two screens? A stylus? It was all very futuristic. And who can forget the thrill of yelling at your DS in public to play Nintendogs?

9. Sega Nomad

Sega Genesis in your hands. That's it; that's the pitch. With the Sega Nomad, you could play actual Genesis cartridges on the go. Battery life? We don't talk about that.

10. Game and Watch Series

Last but not least, we have Nintendo's Game and Watch series. These adorable little devices were the predecessors to the Game Boy, and each one had a single game. They were like Pringles; once you pop, you can't stop.

And there you have it, folks! The top 10 retro gaming handhelds you absolutely must own in 2023. Sure, they might not have the pixel-perfect screens or battery life of today's devices, and some of them might actually weigh more than a small child, but hey, that's all part of the charm, right? Now, if you'll excuse me, I have a date with some Double A batteries and my Game Gear.
