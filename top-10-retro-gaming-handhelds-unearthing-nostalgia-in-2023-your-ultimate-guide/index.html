Top 10 Retro Gaming Handhelds: Unearthing Nostalgia in 2023 - Your Ultimate Guide

Ah, 2023. Self-driving cars have become a thing, aliens still haven't made contact, and we're making meals in capsules. The future is all very shiny and new, right?

But let's face it, sometimes you just want to feel the cramp in your thumbs, the strain in your eyes, and the sweet, sweet taste of nostalgia that only a retro gaming handheld can deliver.

So, put on your rose-colored glasses, sit back, and let's take a delightful romp through the top 10 retro gaming handhelds that will transport you back to simpler times.

1. Game Boy (1989)

Remember the days when 8-bit graphics were the height of innovation? Ah, good times. The Nintendo Game Boy is the OG of gaming handhelds. Who needs color when you've got four shades of greenish-gray? It was all about Tetris, Super Mario Land, and The Legend of Zelda. But let's not forget about the good old-fashioned AA batteries that powered our countless hours of fun. Sustainable? Maybe not. Iconic? Absolutely.

2. Sega Game Gear (1990)

Ah, Sega Game Gear, the Game Boy's technicolor cousin. This was one gorgeous piece of kit. Sega aimed to outdo Game Boy with a full-color screen and a slick design. Sonic the Hedgehog and Streets of Rage made their way into our pockets. But a word of warning: this little beast was a battery guzzler. Six AA batteries for a measly 3-4 hours of gameplay? Talk about high maintenance.

3. Game Boy Color (1998)

Nintendo struck back with the Game Boy Color, allowing us to finally play Pokémon in something other than monochrome. The memories of trading Pokémons via cable in the schoolyard are coming back, aren't they? It had a good battery life and a decent library of games—what more could we ask for?

4. Atari Lynx (1989)

Atari Lynx was the cool kid on the block. Backlit color screen? Check. Ambidextrous layout? Check. But the games... Oh boy, the games. Who could forget the likes of Blue Lightning and California Games? We may not have had many titles, but the ones we had were chef's kiss perfection.

5. Game Boy Advance (2001)

The Game Boy Advance was a Herculean leap forward. It had a wider screen, shoulder buttons, and could play all previous Game Boy games. Remember sitting under a lamp just to see your game because of the non-lit screen? Yeah, fun times. But hey, at least we got to play amazing titles like Advance Wars.

6. Neo Geo Pocket Color (1999)

The Neo Geo Pocket Color, with its clicky joystick and sturdy build, was a fighting game fan's dream. Games like King of Fighters R-2 and Samurai Shodown made us feel like we had an arcade in our pockets. So what if it didn't have the massive library of the Game Boy Color? Quality over quantity, folks.

7. Nintendo DS (2004)

Hello, dual-screens! The Nintendo DS was a game-changer (quite literally). Touchscreen, Wi-Fi, voice recognition—Nintendo was thinking way ahead of its time. The likes of Mario Kart DS and Pokémon HeartGold and SoulSilver kept us glued to our DS, much to the chagrin of our parents/siblings/roommates.

8. PlayStation Portable (2004)

The PlayStation Portable (or PSP to its friends) was a powerhouse. It felt like having a PS2 that you could carry around. Grand Theft Auto, God of War, and Final Fantasy at our fingertips? Yes please! And then there was the UMD disc... which was, erm, let's call it "unique."

9. WonderSwan Color (2000)

WonderSwan Color, oh you elusive creature. This Japan-exclusive handheld had a great mix of titles, including Final Fantasy remakes and Digimon games. It didn't take off globally, but it definitely left its mark.

10. Game Boy Micro (2005)

And finally, we arrive at the Game Boy Micro. The tiniest member of the Game Boy family, it was actually quite nifty. Its backlit screen and customizable faceplates were cool touches. It was essentially a pocket-sized Game Boy Advance, even if it didn't take off quite as well.

And there you have it, folks. An emotional rollercoaster ride through the world of retro handhelds!

Are they clunky? Yes. Are they impractical by today's standards? Absolutely. But would we trade them for the world's fanciest VR headset? Not on your life.

Here's to the joy of sore thumbs, 8-bit music, and the simple pleasure of blowing into a cartridge to make the game work. Because let's face it, the future might be shiny and new, but the past...that's where the fun really is.
